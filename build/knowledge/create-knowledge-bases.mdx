## Overview

Creating a knowledge base is your first step toward giving your AI agent expert-level knowledge of your business. This guide walks you through the complete process, from creating the knowledge base to organizing content and following best practices.

<Tip>
Plan your knowledge structure before you start. A well-organized knowledge base is easier to maintain and more effective for your agents.
</Tip>

---

## Step 1: Create a Knowledge Base

### Navigate to Knowledge Bases

1. Log into your itellicoAI dashboard at [app.itellico.ai](https://app.itellico.ai)
2. Click **Knowledge Bases** in the left navigation menu
3. Click the **+ Create Knowledge Base** button

### Fill in Basic Information

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enter Knowledge Base Name">
    Choose a clear, descriptive name that reflects the content category.

    **Naming rules:**
    - Letters, numbers, spaces, hyphens, and underscores only
    - No special characters (@, #, $, etc.)
    - Maximum 100 characters

    **Good examples:**
    - "Customer Support FAQ"
    - "Product Documentation"
    - "Company Policies"
    - "Technical Troubleshooting"

    **Avoid:**
    - "KB_001" (not descriptive)
    - "Misc Docs" (too vague)
    - "Everything!!!" (unprofessional)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add Description (Optional)">
    Provide context about what this knowledge base contains and when to use it.

    **Example descriptions:**
    - "Customer-facing support documentation including FAQs, policies, and troubleshooting guides"
    - "Internal product specifications, feature documentation, and technical details"
    - "Company HR policies, compliance documents, and internal procedures"
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Create">
    Your knowledge base is now created and ready for content!
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Knowledge bases are available to agents once you link them in agent configuration.
</Note>

## Step 2: Create Folders

Folders organize your knowledge items into logical groups.

### Add Your First Folder

1. Open your newly created knowledge base
2. Click the **Create Folder** button
3. Enter a folder name
4. Add an optional description
5. Click **Create**

### Folder Naming Best Practices

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Clear & Specific" icon="check">
    - "Billing & Payments"
    - "Return Policies"
    - "Product Specifications"
    - "Technical Issues"
  </Card>
  <Card title="Vague or Generic" icon="xmark">
    - "Stuff"
    - "Folder 1"
    - "Docs"
    - "Misc"
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### How Many Folders?

<Accordion title="Small Knowledge Base (< 50 items)">
  **3-5 folders** is usually sufficient

  **Example structure:**
  - Customer Questions
  - Company Policies
  - Product Information
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Medium Knowledge Base (50-200 items)">
  **5-10 folders** provides good organization

  **Example structure:**
  - Billing & Payments
  - Technical Support
  - Product Features
  - Account Management
  - Shipping & Returns
  - General FAQ
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Large Knowledge Base (200+ items)">
  **10-15 folders** keeps things manageable

  Consider splitting into multiple knowledge bases if you exceed 15 folders.

  **Example structure:**
  - Account Setup
  - Billing Issues
  - Payment Methods
  - Refunds & Credits
  - Product Categories A-F
  - Product Categories G-M
  - Product Categories N-Z
  - Technical Troubleshooting
  - Integration Guides
  - API Documentation
  - Returns & Exchanges
  - Shipping Options
  - Order Tracking
</Accordion>

## Step 3: Add Knowledge Items

Knowledge items are the actual content - the documents, FAQs, policies, and information you want your agent to know.

### Choose Content Type

When you click **Add Knowledge Item**, you'll select a content type:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Text Item">
    **Best for:** FAQs, policies, procedures you write directly

    Direct text entry using the built-in editor. Perfect for content you're creating specifically for your knowledge base.

    **Use cases:**
    - FAQ answers
    - Policy summaries
    - Step-by-step procedures
    - Quick reference information
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="File Upload">
    **Best for:** Existing documents, manuals, detailed guides

    Upload documents in various formats (PDF, DOC, DOCX, TXT) up to 10MB. The system will extract and process the text content.

    **Use cases:**
    - User manuals
    - Product specifications
    - Legal documents
    - Training materials
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="URL">
    **Best for:** Web pages, online documentation, public resources

    Provide a URL and the system will scrape and process the web page content.

    **Use cases:**
    - Public documentation pages
    - Blog posts
    - Help center articles
    - External resources
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Add a Text Item

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click 'Add Knowledge Item'">
    In your collection, click the add button to create a new item.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select 'TEXT' as category">
    Choose text if you're entering content directly.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter a descriptive title">
    **Good titles:**
    - "Return Policy for Digital Products"
    - "Password Reset Instructions"
    - "Pricing Tiers Explained"

    **Avoid:**
    - "Doc 1"
    - "Policy"
    - "Info"
  </Step>

  <Step title="Write or paste your content">
    Enter the information you want your agent to know. Be clear and comprehensive.

    **Example:**
    ```
    Return Policy for Digital Products

    Digital products can be refunded within 30 days of purchase if:
    1. The product has a technical defect that prevents usage
    2. The product description was materially inaccurate
    3. The customer has not accessed or downloaded the product

    Refunds are not available for:
    - Change of mind after accessing the product
    - Compatibility issues that were disclosed in product description
    - User error or misunderstanding of features

    To request a refund, customers should email support@company.com
    with their order number and reason for the refund request.

    Processing time: 5-7 business days
    ```
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click 'Create'">
    Your knowledge item is saved and will be processed immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Add a File

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click 'Add Knowledge Item'">
    In your folder, click the add button.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select 'FILE' as category">
    Choose file for document uploads.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter a title">
    Give your file a descriptive title (the filename will be stored separately).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Upload your file">
    Click **Upload** and select your document.

    **Requirements:**
    - File formats: PDF, DOC, DOCX, TXT, and other document formats
    - Maximum size: 10MB
    - Text-based documents and scanned images (advanced parsing handles most scans)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click 'Create'">
    The system will upload and begin processing your file. Status will change from PENDING → PROCESSING → COMPLETED.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
File processing time varies based on file size and complexity. Large files (5-10MB) may take several minutes to process.
</Warning>

### Add a URL

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click 'Add Knowledge Item'">
    In your collection, click the add button.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select 'URL' as category">
    Choose URL to scrape web content.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter a title">
    Give the content a descriptive title.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter the source URL">
    Paste the complete URL including https://

    **Example:**
    ```
    https://docs.company.com/api/authentication
    ```
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click 'Create'">
    The system will fetch and process the web page content. Status will change from PENDING → PROCESSING → COMPLETED.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
URL scraping works best with simple, text-based web pages. Complex JavaScript applications or pages requiring authentication may not scrape successfully.
</Note>

## Step 4: Organize Your Content

### Logical Grouping

Organize items into folders based on topic, function, or user needs:

**Example: Customer Support Knowledge Base**

**Account & Login folder (12 items):**
- How to Create an Account
- Password Reset Process
- Two-Factor Authentication Setup
- Account Recovery Options

**Billing & Payments folder (18 items):**
- Payment Methods Accepted
- Update Payment Information
- Refund Policy - General
- Refund Policy - Digital Products

**Technical Support folder (34 items):**
- Troubleshooting Connection Issues
- Browser Compatibility
- Common Error Messages

**Shipping & Returns folder (15 items):**
- Shipping Options & Costs
- International Shipping
- Return Shipping Instructions

### Move Items Between Folders

If you need to reorganize:

1. Edit the knowledge item
2. Change the folder assignment
3. Save changes

<Tip>
It's okay to reorganize as you learn what structure works best. Start with a reasonable structure and refine as you go.
</Tip>

### Delete or Archive Outdated Content

Keep your knowledge base current:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Edit to Update" icon="pen">
    If information changes, edit the existing item rather than creating a new one.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Delete Obsolete Items" icon="trash">
    Remove items that are no longer relevant or accurate.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

  </Tab>

### Folder Names

Use consistent patterns:

**Pattern 1: Topic-based**
- Account Management
- Billing & Payments
- Technical Issues
- Product Features

**Pattern 2: Question-based**
- How Do I...?
- What Is...?
- Troubleshooting
- Policies & Rules

- Advanced Topics

Make titles descriptive and searchable:

## Content Quality Guidelines

### Write Clear, Comprehensive Content

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Be specific and detailed" icon="magnifying-glass">
    Don't assume knowledge. Explain clearly.

    **Instead of:**
    "Customers can return items within our standard window."

    **Write:**
    "Customers can return items within 30 days of purchase. Items must be unused and in original packaging with all tags attached."
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Use plain language" icon="comment">
    Avoid jargon unless it's industry-standard and your audience knows it.

    **Instead of:**
    "Utilize the authentication endpoint to generate a bearer token."

    **Write:**
    "To authenticate, call the /auth endpoint. You'll receive an access token to include in subsequent requests."
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Include examples" icon="list-check">
    Examples make abstract concepts concrete.

    **Example:**
    "Our pricing tiers include:
    - Basic: $29/month - Up to 1,000 contacts
    - Pro: $99/month - Up to 10,000 contacts
    - Enterprise: Custom pricing - Unlimited contacts"
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Structure with headings" icon="heading">
    Use headings and bullet points for scannable content.

    **Good structure:**
    ```
    Return Policy

    Eligible Items:
    - Unopened products in original packaging
    - Items purchased within 30 days
    - Products without signs of use

    Non-Eligible Items:
    - Digital downloads
    - Personalized products
    - Sale items marked as final sale

    Process:
    1. Email support@company.com
    2. Provide order number
    3. Receive return authorization
    4. Ship item within 7 days
    ```
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Keep content focused" icon="bullseye">
    Each item should cover one topic thoroughly. Split large topics into multiple items.

    **Instead of:**
    One 20-page "Complete Company Handbook"

    **Create:**
    - Return Policy
    - Shipping Options
    - Payment Methods
    - Product Warranty
    - Privacy Policy
    - Contact Information
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Link Knowledge Base to Agents

Once you've added content, link your knowledge base to agents:

<Steps>

    Check that items are organized properly and content is accurate.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Test a few items">

  </Step>

  <Step title="Assign to agents">

  </Step>

## Common Issues & Solutions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="File processing failed">
    **Possible causes:**
    - File exceeds 10MB limit
    - Very poor quality scanned images
    - File is corrupted or password-protected

    **Solutions:**
    - Compress file or split into smaller files
    - For very poor quality scans, convert to text and upload as TEXT item instead
    - Remove password protection
    - Convert to text and upload as TEXT item instead
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="URL scraping failed">
    **Possible causes:**
    - Page requires authentication
    - Content loads via JavaScript
    - URL is incorrect or broken
    - Website blocks scraping

    **Solutions:**
    - Copy page content manually and create TEXT item
    - Check URL is publicly accessible
    - Use direct URL to content, not homepage
    - For complex sites, export content as PDF
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Too many items to manage">
    **Solution:**
    - Split into multiple knowledge bases by topic
    - Create more granular folders
    - Delete outdated items
    - Consider if all content is necessary

    **Ideal sizes:**
    - Folder: 10-30 items
    - Knowledge Base: 50-200 items total
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Agent isn't using my knowledge">
    **Check:**
    - Is knowledge base assigned to agent?
    - Is correct access mode selected (Context vs RAG)?
    - Are items in COMPLETED status?

    See [Context vs RAG](/build/knowledge/context-vs-rag) for integration details.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Best Practices Checklist

Before linking your knowledge base to agents, verify:

- [ ] Knowledge base has a clear, descriptive name
- [ ] Folders are logically organized by topic
- [ ] Item titles are specific and searchable
- [ ] Content is written clearly and comprehensively
- [ ] All items show COMPLETED status
- [ ] No duplicate or conflicting information
- [ ] Outdated content has been removed
- [ ] Content has been reviewed for accuracy
