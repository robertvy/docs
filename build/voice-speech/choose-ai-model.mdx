## Overview

The AI model (LLM) is the brain of your voice agent. It processes what customers say, understands their intent, reasons about the best response, and decides when to take actions. Choosing the right model means balancing performance, latency, cost, and compliance requirements.

<Note>
Model selection happens under **Models > Model** in your agent configuration. Changes apply immediately—no separate publish step required.
</Note>

## Understanding Language Models

Language models are trained on vast amounts of text to understand and generate human language. In voice agents, the LLM interprets customer requests, reasons about the best response based on your instructions and knowledge base, decides when to use actions like transfers or bookings, generates natural conversation responses, and maintains context throughout the conversation.

Different models excel at different tasks. Some prioritize speed, others accuracy, and some offer the best balance for conversational AI.

## Available Model Providers

### OpenAI

OpenAI models are among the most popular for conversational AI, offering strong performance and reliability.

**GPT-4.1**
- Best for: Complex reasoning, multi-step tasks, high-accuracy requirements
- Strengths: Excellent instruction following, strong context understanding
- Considerations: Higher latency than smaller models, higher cost
- Typical use: Customer support with complex troubleshooting, sales conversations

**GPT-4o (Optimized)**
- Best for: Balance of speed and capability
- Strengths: Faster than GPT-4.1 while maintaining quality
- Considerations: Still higher cost tier
- Typical use: Most general-purpose voice agents

**GPT-3.5 Turbo**
- Best for: High-volume, straightforward conversations
- Strengths: Fast, cost-effective, reliable
- Considerations: Less capable with complex reasoning
- Typical use: FAQ answering, simple routing, data collection

### Anthropic

Anthropic's Claude models excel at following detailed instructions and maintaining safety guardrails.

**Claude Sonnet 4.5**
- Best for: Most voice agent use cases, conversational nuance, instruction adherence
- Strengths: Excellent at following complex behavioral rules, strong context retention, reliable tool calling
- Considerations: Moderate latency
- Typical use: Customer support, sales qualification, appointment booking, agents with detailed personality requirements

### Groq

Groq offers ultra-fast inference with open-source models, ideal for latency-sensitive applications.

**Llama 3.1 (70B/405B)**
- Best for: Open-source preference, low-latency requirements
- Strengths: Near-instant response generation, competitive quality
- Considerations: May require more prompt engineering than proprietary models
- Typical use: Real-time conversational agents where every millisecond counts

**Mixtral 8x7B**
- Best for: Cost-conscious deployments with speed requirements
- Strengths: Excellent speed-to-cost ratio
- Considerations: Smaller context window than top-tier models
- Typical use: High-volume screening calls, basic qualification

### Azure OpenAI

Azure-hosted OpenAI models with enterprise compliance and regional deployment options.

**Azure GPT-4 / GPT-3.5**
- Best for: Enterprise customers, EU data residency requirements
- Strengths: Same models as OpenAI with Azure compliance, EU hosting available
- Considerations: May have regional availability limits
- Typical use: Enterprise deployments with compliance requirements

<Note>
EU-hosted models provide additional data residency guarantees for customers with strict regional requirements. All providers have Data Processing Agreements (DPAs) in place for GDPR compliance.
</Note>

## Model Parameters

Click **Model Parameters** to access advanced configuration options that control how the model behaves.

### Temperature

Controls randomness in responses (range: 0.0 to 2.0)

- **0.0 (Recommended):** Deterministic, consistent responses
  - Use for: Most voice agents, tool calling, action execution
  - Maximizes reliability for transfers, bookings, and API calls
  - Ensures consistent behavior and predictable responses

- **0.1 - 0.3:** Slightly varied but still highly consistent
  - Use for: Agents that need slight natural variation
  - Still reliable for tool calling

- **0.4 - 0.7:** More creative and varied
  - Use for: Personality-driven agents where creativity matters more than consistency
  - Tool calling reliability decreases

- **0.8+:** Highly creative, unpredictable
  - Avoid for production voice agents
  - Tool calling becomes unreliable

<Note>
**Default recommendation:** Use 0.0 unless your agent needs more human-like creativity. Temperature above 0 reduces tool calling reliability (transfers, bookings, actions).
</Note>

### Max Tokens

Maximum number of tokens the model can generate per response (range varies by model)

- **Short (50-100 tokens):** Quick, concise responses
  - Best for: Fast-paced conversations, simple confirmations
  - Equivalent to: ~1-2 sentences or ~8-15 seconds of speech

- **Medium (100-200 tokens):** Standard conversational responses
  - Best for: Most voice agents, detailed explanations
  - Equivalent to: ~2-4 sentences or ~15-30 seconds of speech

- **Long (200-300 tokens):** Extended explanations
  - Best for: Complex troubleshooting, detailed product information
  - Risk: Customer disengagement, interruptions

<Warning>
For voice, responses over 200 tokens (~30 seconds) risk customer frustration. Most responses should be under 150 tokens (~20 seconds). Use instructions to enforce brevity instead of relying solely on token limits.
</Warning>

### Top P (Nucleus Sampling)

Alternative to temperature, controls diversity by selecting from top probability tokens (range: 0.0 to 1.0)

- **0.9-1.0:** Consider all likely options (standard)
- **0.5-0.8:** More focused, deterministic outputs
- Use temperature OR top_p, not both simultaneously

### Frequency Penalty

Reduces repetition by penalizing tokens that have already appeared (range: 0.0 to 2.0)

- **0.0:** No penalty (default)
- **0.5-1.0:** Discourages repetitive phrases
- **1.5+:** Strongly avoids repetition (may affect coherence)

Use when your agent tends to repeat phrases like "I understand" or "Of course" too frequently.

### Presence Penalty

Encourages introducing new topics by penalizing any token that has appeared (range: 0.0 to 2.0)

- **0.0:** No encouragement for new topics
- **0.5-1.0:** Gentle push toward topic diversity
- **1.5+:** Strong push for new content

Less useful in voice agents where customers control topic flow.

### Stop Sequences

Custom strings that halt generation when encountered. Useful for structured outputs or preventing unwanted continuations.

Example: Stop at `"\n\nHuman:"` to prevent the model from simulating customer responses.

### Response Format

Some models support structured output (JSON mode):
- **Text:** Standard conversational output (default for voice)
- **JSON:** Structured data output (use only when parsing responses programmatically)

### Streaming

Controls whether responses stream token-by-token or arrive complete:
- **Enabled:** Agent starts speaking as soon as first tokens arrive (lower perceived latency)
- **Disabled:** Agent waits for complete response before speaking (more reliable for complex logic)

<Note>
Streaming is recommended for voice agents to minimize perceived latency and create natural conversation flow.
</Note>

## Choosing the Right Model

### Decision Framework

Use this framework to select your model:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="1. Define Your Requirements" icon="list-check">
    **Accuracy Requirements:**
    - High accuracy needed? → GPT-4.1, Claude Sonnet 4.5
    - Moderate accuracy sufficient? → GPT-4o, Llama 3.1

    **Latency Requirements:**
    - Ultra-low latency critical? → Groq models (Llama, Mixtral)
    - Moderate latency acceptable? → GPT-4o, Claude Sonnet 4.5
    - Latency less important than quality? → GPT-4.1, Claude Sonnet 4.5

    **Cost Constraints:**
    - Budget-conscious? → GPT-3.5 Turbo, Mixtral
    - Balanced? → GPT-4o, Llama 3.1 70B
    - Premium quality? → GPT-4.1, Claude Sonnet 4.5
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="2. Consider Compliance" icon="shield">
    **EU Data Residency:**
    - Required? → Filter for EU-hosted models (Azure, select providers)
    - Not required? → All models available

    **Industry Regulations:**
    - Enterprise compliance needs? → Contact support
    - Financial services? → Consider Claude for instruction adherence
    - General commercial? → All models appropriate
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="3. Assess Conversation Complexity" icon="brain">
    **Simple Routing / FAQ:**
    - GPT-3.5 Turbo, Mixtral 8x7B

    **Standard Customer Support:**
    - GPT-4o, Llama 3.1 70B, Claude Sonnet 4.5

    **Complex Reasoning / Multi-Step:**
    - GPT-4.1, Claude Sonnet 4.5, Llama 3.1 405B

    **Personality-Critical / Brand-Sensitive:**
    - Claude Sonnet 4.5, GPT-4.1
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="4. Factor in Volume" icon="chart-line">
    **Low Volume (under 100 calls/day):**
    - Choose best quality: GPT-4.1, Claude Sonnet 4.5

    **Medium Volume (100-1,000 calls/day):**
    - Balance quality and cost: GPT-4o, Llama 3.1 70B

    **High Volume (1,000+ calls/day):**
    - Optimize for cost: GPT-3.5 Turbo, Mixtral
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Testing Model Performance

### A/B Testing Models

To compare models scientifically:

1. **Duplicate your agent** in the dashboard
2. **Change only the model** on one version
3. **Keep all other settings identical** (instructions, voice, actions)
4. **Run identical test scenarios** on both
5. **Compare:**
   - Response quality and accuracy
   - Latency and speed
   - Conversation naturalness
   - Action trigger reliability

### Evaluation Criteria

Rate each model on:

| Criteria | What to Look For |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Accuracy** | Does it understand requests correctly? |
| **Instruction Adherence** | Does it follow your system prompt rules? |
| **Latency** | How quickly does it respond? |
| **Context Retention** | Does it remember earlier conversation? |
| **Action Timing** | Does it trigger actions at right moments? |
| **Error Handling** | How does it handle unclear requests? |
| **Cost** | Is the quality improvement worth the price difference? |

### Test Scenarios

Create a standard test set covering:
- **Common requests** (80% of real conversations)
- **Edge cases** (unclear requests, interruptions)
- **Multi-turn conversations** (context retention)
- **Action triggers** (booking, transfer scenarios)
- **Error conditions** (API failures, missing data)

Run the same set on each model for fair comparison.

## Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Start with Recommended Defaults" icon="rocket">
    For most voice agents, start with:
    - **Model:** GPT-4o or Claude Sonnet 4.5
    - **Temperature:** 0.7
    - **Max Tokens:** 250
    - **Streaming:** Enabled

    Adjust based on testing results.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Match Model to Complexity" icon="scale-balanced">
    Don't over-engineer simple use cases:
    - FAQ answering doesn't need GPT-4.1
    - Complex multi-step reasoning doesn't work well with minimal models

    Match capability to requirement.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Monitor Real-World Performance" icon="chart-line">
    Use analytics to track:
    - Average response time
    - Action success rates
    - Transfer rates (high transfers may indicate reasoning issues)
    - Customer satisfaction scores

    Switch models if metrics degrade.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Consider Regional Deployment" icon="globe">
    If serving global customers:
    - Use EU-hosted models for European callers (GDPR)
    - Consider regional Azure deployments for enterprise compliance

  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Document Model Changes" icon="file-pen">
    When changing models in production:
    - Note the date and reason in agent description
    - Monitor metrics for 24-48 hours after
    - Keep previous model ID documented for rollback
    - Test thoroughly before switching high-volume agents
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Troubleshooting Model Issues

### Agent Responses Are Too Verbose

**Solutions:**
- Lower max tokens (try 150-200)
- Add to instructions: "Keep every response under 25 seconds"
- Use temperature 0.0 for more focused, concise responses
- Consider faster model that encourages brevity

### Agent Misunderstands Requests

**Solutions:**
- Switch to higher-capability model (GPT-4.1, Claude Sonnet 4.5)
- Improve instructions with more specific examples
- Add keyword boosting in transcriber settings
- Review transcription accuracy first (may be STT issue, not LLM)

### Agent Doesn't Follow Instructions

**Solutions:**
- Claude models typically better at instruction adherence
- Simplify and clarify instructions
- Use bulleted lists instead of paragraphs
- Add explicit examples of correct behavior
- Use temperature 0.0 for maximum consistency

### High Latency / Slow Responses

**Solutions:**
- Switch to faster model (Groq, GPT-3.5)
- Reduce max tokens
- Enable streaming if disabled
- Check if issue is model or network latency (test with different providers)

### Agent Repeats Same Phrases

**Solutions:**
- Increase frequency penalty (0.5-1.0)
- Add instruction: "Vary your phrasing; avoid repetitive expressions"
- Consider different model (some have better diversity)
- Review if instructions inadvertently cause repetition

## Model Updates and Versioning

### Provider Model Updates

Model providers regularly update their offerings:
- **Minor updates** often improve performance without breaking changes
- **Major version changes** (e.g., GPT-4 → GPT-5) may require testing
- itellicoAI notifies customers before automatic version updates

### Controlling Model Versions

Some providers let you pin to specific versions:
- **Latest:** Always use newest version (default, recommended)
- **Pinned:** Stay on specific version (use if you've heavily optimized for that model)

### Deprecation Policy

When providers deprecate models:
1. itellicoAI emails affected customers 60 days before sunset
2. Recommended migration path provided
3. Agents automatically moved to successor model if no action taken
4. Migration assistance available from support

## Pricing and Cost Management

### Understanding Model Costs

Models charge per token (input + output):
- **Input tokens:** Your instructions + conversation history + knowledge
- **Output tokens:** Agent's generated responses

Costs vary widely:
- Budget tier: $0.50-$2 per million tokens
- Mid tier: $5-$15 per million tokens
- Premium tier: $20-$60 per million tokens

### Cost Optimization Tips

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Minimize Instruction Length" icon="scissors">
    Shorter instructions = lower input token count per turn
  </Card>
  <Card title="Use Knowledge Efficiently" icon="book">
    RAG mode only injects relevant knowledge, reducing tokens
  </Card>
  <Card title="Limit Max Tokens" icon="gauge">
    Cap output tokens to prevent unnecessarily long responses
  </Card>
  <Card title="Choose Appropriate Model" icon="bullseye">
    Don't use GPT-4 for tasks GPT-3.5 can handle
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Estimating Monthly Costs

Example calculation for 1,000 calls/month:
```text wrap
Average conversation: 15 turns
Average response: 150 tokens
Input per turn: 1,500 tokens (instructions + history)
Output per turn: 150 tokens

Per conversation:
- Input: 15 turns × 1,500 tokens = 22,500 tokens
- Output: 15 turns × 150 tokens = 2,250 tokens

Total monthly tokens (1,000 calls):
- Input: 22.5M tokens
- Output: 2.25M tokens

With GPT-4o ($5/M input, $15/M output):
- Input cost: 22.5M × $5/M = $112.50
- Output cost: 2.25M × $15/M = $33.75
- Total: $146.25/month for model usage

With GPT-3.5 Turbo ($1/M input, $2/M output):
- Total: $27/month for model usage
```text wrap

Cost tracking available in Analytics > Usage Reports.
