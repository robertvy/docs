## Overview

Your agent's voice is a critical part of the customer experience. The right voice can build trust, convey professionalism, and align with your brand identity. itellicoAI currently streams live catalogs from ElevenLabs, Microsoft Azure Neural voices, and Cartesia so you can choose high-quality audio without manual uploads.

<Note>
Voice selection happens under the **Voice** tab in your agent configuration. You can preview any voice before committing, and changes apply immediately.
</Note>

## Voice Providers

### ElevenLabs

ElevenLabs provides AI-generated voices with exceptional naturalness and emotional range.

**Characteristics:**
- **Ultra-realistic:** Nearly indistinguishable from human speech
- **Emotional range:** Can convey empathy, enthusiasm, professionalism
- **Consistent quality:** Every sentence sounds natural, not robotic
- **Low latency:** Fast generation for real-time conversations

**Best for:**
- Customer-facing agents where voice quality is paramount
- Brand-sensitive applications
- Agents requiring emotional intelligence
- Use cases where the voice represents your company identity

**Popular ElevenLabs voices:**
- **Rachel:** Warm, professional American female
- **Adam:** Confident, clear American male
- **Bella:** Friendly, approachable female
- **Antoni:** Calm, reassuring male

**Considerations:**
- Premium pricing tier
- Some voices are ElevenLabs exclusive
- Exceptional quality justifies cost for high-value conversations

<Note>
ElevenLabs voices support advanced settings like stability and similarity boost—configure these in Voice Settings.
</Note>

### Azure Speech (Neural Voices)

Microsoft Azure offers a vast catalog of neural voices across dozens of languages and locales.

**Characteristics:**
- **Extensive language coverage:** 100+ languages and locales
- **Consistent pricing:** Predictable, enterprise-friendly
- **Regional options:** Can be hosted in EU, US, Asia
- **Reliable quality:** Professional-grade, consistent output

**Best for:**
- Multilingual agents (one provider for all languages)
- Enterprise deployments requiring compliance
- High-volume applications with cost constraints
- Agents serving global markets

**Popular Azure voices:**
- **en-US-JennyNeural:** Natural American female, versatile
- **en-GB-SoniaNeural:** British female, professional
- **es-ES-ElviraNeural:** Spanish female, clear
- **de-DE-KatjaNeural:** German female, authoritative

**Voice Categories:**
- **Standard Neural:** High-quality, cost-effective
- **Neural HD:** Enhanced quality for premium applications
- **Multilingual:** Single voice supporting multiple languages
- **Custom Neural:** Train voices on your audio (enterprise only)

**Considerations:**
- Quality slightly below ElevenLabs for emotional nuance
- Excellent for factual, professional conversations
- Best-in-class language and locale coverage

### Cartesia

Cartesia focuses on ultra-low-latency streaming and a modern sound tuned for conversational AI.

**Characteristics:**
- **Realtime friendly:** Optimized for sub-second turn taking, ideal for interactive agents.
- **Expressive controls:** Voices lean toward energetic, lifelike deliveries that keep users engaged.
- **EU hosting:** Audio stays within Cartesia’s EU region when required.

**Best for:**
- Web experiences where latency and expressiveness matter more than strict brand matching.
- Teams that want a second style alongside ElevenLabs for A/B testing.

**Considerations:**
- Smaller catalog than Azure or ElevenLabs (today it focuses on English and a handful of multilingual options).
- No fine-grained sliders yet; treat it as a “pick the vibe and go” provider.

## Selecting the Right Voice

### Voice Selection Framework

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="1. Define Your Brand Voice" icon="palette">
    Before browsing voices, define your brand's voice characteristics:

    **Tone:**
    - Professional and authoritative
    - Friendly and approachable
    - Empathetic and caring
    - Energetic and enthusiastic
    - Calm and reassuring

    **Personality:**
    - Formal vs. casual
    - Reserved vs. outgoing
    - Serious vs. playful
    - Technical vs. accessible

    **Example:**
    "Our healthcare agent should sound empathetic, professional, and reassuring—not too clinical, but authoritative enough to build trust."
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="2. Consider Your Audience" icon="users">
    Match voice to customer expectations:

    **Age demographics:**
    - Younger audiences: More casual, energetic voices
    - Older audiences: Clear, slower-paced, professional voices
    - Mixed ages: Neutral, universally accessible voice

    **Industry context:**
    - Legal/Medical: Authoritative, professional
    - Retail/Hospitality: Friendly, helpful
    - Tech Support: Patient, clear, knowledgeable
    - Sales: Confident, enthusiastic

    **Geographic considerations:**
    - Local accent preferences (British English for UK customers)
    - Neutral accents for global audiences
    - Regional dialects for local businesses
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="3. Test with Real Scenarios" icon="vial">
    Don't choose based on preview samples alone:

    **Test process:**
    1. Shortlist 3-5 voices that match your criteria
    2. Configure each on a test agent
    3. Run realistic conversation scenarios
    4. Have team members call and evaluate
    5. Consider customer feedback if possible

    **Evaluation criteria:**
    - Does it match your brand?
    - Is it pleasant to listen to for 5+ minutes?
    - Does it handle your industry terminology well?
    - Is it clear and easily understood?
    - Does it sound natural in longer conversations?
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="4. Factor in Technical Requirements" icon="gear">
    **EU compliance:**
    - If serving European customers, prioritize EU-hosted voices (GDPR)
    - Azure offers EU regions for most voices
    - Check for EU badge in voice metadata

    **Language requirements:**
    - Multilingual agents: Use provider with broad language coverage (Azure)
    - Single language: Optimize for quality (ElevenLabs)
    - Accent-specific needs: Filter by region/locale

    **Performance:**
    - Low latency critical: Groq/Deepgram voices, or ElevenLabs Turbo
    - Quality paramount: ElevenLabs standard models
    - High volume cost-sensitive: Azure standard neural voices
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Gender and Inclusive Voice Selection

### Gender Options

Voices are categorized by gender presentation:
- **Female voices:** Typically perceived as approachable, empathetic
- **Male voices:** Often perceived as authoritative, confident
- **Neutral/androgynous voices:** Non-binary options available from select providers

<Note>
Gender associations are stereotypes and vary by culture. Test with your specific audience rather than assuming preferences.
</Note>

### Research on Voice Gender

Studies show mixed results on voice gender preferences:
- Some industries see higher trust with female voices (healthcare, education)
- Some contexts prefer male voices (security, finance)
- Many audiences have no strong preference
- **Recommendation:** Test both genders with your audience and measure satisfaction

### Avoiding Bias

Consider these approaches:
- **Rotate voices:** Use different voices for different contact segments
- **Let customers choose:** Offer voice selection in IVR menu
- **Focus on quality:** Choose the most natural, clear voice regardless of gender
- **Match context:** Select based on use case, not assumptions

## Voice Localization and Accents

### Accent Considerations

Accent can significantly impact customer experience:

**Local accents:**
- Build rapport with local customers
- May sound more trustworthy or relatable
- Can be harder to understand for non-locals

**Neutral accents:**
- Broadly accessible
- Professional and clear
- May lack local connection

**Examples:**
- US business serving New York: Consider New York accent or neutral American
- UK business serving London: British RP or London accent
- Global SaaS: Neutral American or International English

### Multilingual Voice Strategies

**Strategy 1: One Voice per Language**
- Use different voices for English, Spanish, German, etc.
- Simpler to configure
- Each language gets optimized voice

**Strategy 2: Multilingual Voices**
- Some Azure voices support multiple languages
- Consistent voice identity across languages
- Customer hears same "agent" regardless of language
- Useful for customers who switch languages mid-conversation

**Strategy 3: Language Detection + Voice Matching**
- Detect customer language automatically
- Route to agent with appropriate language voice
- Best for complex multilingual environments

## Voice Testing Best Practices

### Testing Process

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="1. Shortlist Phase" icon="list">
    - Browse voice library with filters applied
    - Preview 10-15 voices using hover functionality
    - Narrow to 3-5 finalists based on:
      - Match to brand voice characteristics
      - Clarity and naturalness
      - Appropriate gender/accent for use case
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="2. Isolated Testing" icon="headphones">
    - Configure each finalist voice on test agents
    - Create test scenarios covering:
      - Simple greetings and confirmations
      - Complex explanations (your actual use case)
      - Industry-specific terminology
      - Numbers, dates, addresses, spelling
      - Error handling and apologies
    - Listen in quiet environment with good audio
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="3. Team Evaluation" icon="users">
    - Have 3-5 team members test each voice
    - Use standardized evaluation form:
      - Clarity (1-5)
      - Naturalness (1-5)
      - Brand fit (1-5)
      - Professionalism (1-5)
      - Overall preference (1-5)
    - Discuss results and rationale
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="4. Customer Testing (Optional)" icon="chart-line">
    - Deploy top 2 voices to small customer segment
    - Measure:
      - Satisfaction scores
      - Completion rates
      - Escalation rates
      - Qualitative feedback
    - Choose winner based on data
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Common Voice Issues and Solutions

| Issue | Likely Cause | Solution |
|-------|--------------|----------|
| Voice sounds robotic | Older voice model or wrong provider | Switch to neural/AI voices (ElevenLabs, Azure Neural) |
| Pronunciation errors | Voice not trained on industry terms | Use custom pronunciations feature |
| Too fast or slow | Default speed settings | Adjust speed in Voice Settings (0.8x - 1.2x) |
| Too high/low pitch | Voice doesn't match brand | Try different voice or adjust pitch in settings |
| Unnatural pauses | Issue with text generation or SSML | Review sentence structure in responses |
| Inconsistent quality | Using multiple voices | Standardize on one voice per agent |

## Voice Compliance and Data Privacy

### EU Hosting and GDPR

If serving European customers, EU hosting is critical:

**Why it matters:**
- GDPR requires personal data (including voice) processed within EU
- EU-hosted voices keep audio processing within European data centers
- Avoids cross-border data transfer complexities

**How to identify EU voices:**
- Look for EU badge in voice metadata
- Filter voice library by "EU-hosted only"
- Azure: Choose EU regions (West Europe, North Europe)
- ElevenLabs: Check voice specification for EU availability

<Warning>
Using non-EU voices for European customers may violate GDPR. Always verify hosting location for compliance-sensitive applications.
</Warning>

### Data Processing and Retention

**What happens to audio:**
- Customer speech is sent to speech-to-text provider
- Transcribed text sent to LLM provider
- LLM response sent to voice synthesis provider
- Voice provider generates audio sent to customer

**Data retention:**
- Most providers don't retain audio after generation
- itellicoAI stores conversation transcripts (configurable)
- Voice biometrics not collected or stored
- Check individual provider data policies

**Compliance certifications:**
- Azure: HIPAA, SOC 2, ISO 27001, GDPR
- ElevenLabs: SOC 2, GDPR-compliant processing
- Verify current certifications for your specific needs

## Advanced Voice Selection Topics

### Voice Fatigue

Long conversations can cause "voice fatigue"—customer irritation from hearing the same voice too long.

**Mitigation strategies:**
- Choose more natural, varied voices (ElevenLabs excels here)
- Vary sentence structure and phrasing in instructions
- Adjust stability/similarity in Voice Settings for more variety
- Consider subtle background ambient sound to break monotony

### Brand Voice Consistency

If using multiple agents across different channels:

**Consistency approaches:**
- **Same voice everywhere:** Phone, web, SMS all use same voice identity
- **Channel-specific:** Different voice per channel (phone vs. web)
- **Use-case specific:** Support uses one voice, sales uses another

**Recommendation:** Use the same voice across all channels for strongest brand identity.

### Seasonal or Promotional Voices

Some businesses change voices for:
- Holiday campaigns (warmer, more enthusiastic)
- Promotional periods (energetic, sales-focused)
- Serious communications (professional, measured)

Easy to test and deploy different voices for specific campaigns.

### Custom Voice Development

**Enterprise feature: Train custom voices**

Process:
1. Record 30-60 minutes of script reading by your chosen voice talent
2. Submit audio to provider (ElevenLabs or Azure Custom Neural)
3. Provider trains AI model on the voice (2-4 weeks)
4. Custom voice appears in your library exclusively
5. Use for brand consistency and unique identity

**Cost:** Typically $1,000-$10,000 depending on provider and customization level.

**Best for:** Large enterprises, recognized brand spokespeople, complete brand control.
