## Overview

Ambient sound adds subtle background audio to your agent's voice, making conversations feel more natural and less sterile. From office ambience to café sounds, these audio layers create a sense of presence and reduce the uncanny valley effect of pure AI voices.

<Note>
Access ambient sound settings under **Sound Effects** in your agent configuration. Volume adjustments apply immediately—no separate publish step required.
</Note>

## What is Ambient Sound?

Ambient sound (also called background ambience) is subtle audio that plays behind your agent's voice:

**What it does:**
- Adds environmental context (office, café, street, call center)
- Reduces the "sterile AI" feeling
- Makes pauses feel more natural (not dead silence)
- Creates brand atmosphere and personality

**What it doesn't do:**
- Replace or drown out the agent's voice
- Distract customers from the conversation
- Add unnecessary noise or complexity

When done right, customers barely notice it consciously—but the conversation feels more natural and engaging.

## Available Ambience Types

### Office Environment

**Description:** Gentle office sounds—distant keyboard typing, paper shuffling, faint conversations in background

**Use cases:**
- Corporate customer support agents
- Professional services (accounting, legal, consulting)
- B2B sales agents
- Administrative assistants

**Atmosphere created:**
- Professional and organized
- Busy but not chaotic
- "You're calling a real office" feeling
- Trustworthy and established

**Volume recommendation:** -80% to -60% (very subtle)

### Call Center

**Description:** More active call center ambience—multiple agents talking in background, phones ringing occasionally, moderate activity level

**Use cases:**
- High-volume support centers
- Emergency hotlines
- Order processing centers
- Technical support

**Atmosphere created:**
- Busy, responsive organization
- "We handle lots of customers" credibility
- Energetic and capable
- Real human environment

**Volume recommendation:** -70% to -50% (subtle to moderate)

<Warning>
Call center ambience can feel stressful if too loud. Keep it subtle to avoid creating urgency or pressure for customers.
</Warning>

### Café / Coffee Shop

**Description:** Relaxed café sounds—gentle conversation murmur, coffee machines, light music, comfortable atmosphere

**Use cases:**
- Lifestyle brands
- Hospitality and food service
- Creative agencies
- Casual customer service (retail, e-commerce)

**Atmosphere created:**
- Friendly and approachable
- Relaxed, no pressure
- Human and personable
- Creative, casual vibe

**Volume recommendation:** -75% to -55% (subtle to moderate)

### Street / Outdoor

**Description:** Outdoor urban ambience—distant traffic, occasional footsteps, light city sounds, open air feeling

**Use cases:**
- Delivery services
- Transportation and logistics
- Real estate (outdoor properties)
- Field service businesses

**Atmosphere created:**
- Active and mobile
- "On the ground" authenticity
- Dynamic and responsive
- Connected to the real world

**Volume recommendation:** -85% to -65% (very subtle to subtle)

### Quiet Room (Minimal Ambience)

**Description:** Nearly silent with occasional very subtle room tone—gentle air conditioning hum, faint ambient noise

**Use cases:**
- Professional services requiring focus (medical, legal, financial)
- Sensitive conversations (healthcare, counseling)
- High-stakes sales (luxury, B2B enterprise)
- Any context where ambience would distract

**Atmosphere created:**
- Focused and attentive
- Serious and respectful
- Private and confidential
- All attention on customer

**Volume recommendation:** -95% to -75% (extremely subtle)

### None (No Ambience)

**Description:** Pure AI voice with no background sound

**Use cases:**
- Testing and development
- Environments where any noise is problematic
- Customers explicitly preferring no background sound

**Atmosphere created:**
- Clean and clear
- Pure AI voice (may feel sterile)
- Maximum clarity
- No distractions

**Volume recommendation:** N/A (ambience disabled)

<Tip>
Most agents benefit from subtle ambience. "None" is perfectly functional but can feel uncanny. Try "Quiet Room" at -90% as a minimal compromise.
</Tip>

## Volume Control

### Understanding the Volume Slider

The volume slider controls ambience level relative to the agent's voice:

**Range:** -100% to +100%

**Relative volume:**
- **-100%:** Ambience off (silent)
- **-90%:** Extremely subtle (barely perceptible)
- **-70%:** Subtle (noticeable if listening for it)
- **-50%:** Moderate (clearly present but background)
- **-30%:** Prominent (competitive with voice)
- **0%:** Equal to voice (too loud for most uses)
- **+50% to +100%:** Ambience louder than voice (rarely appropriate)

<Note>
Volume is relative to voice level, not absolute. If you adjust voice volume elsewhere, ambience adjusts proportionally.
</Note>

### Recommended Volume Settings

**By use case:**

| Use Case | Recommended Volume | Rationale |
|----------|-------------------|-----------|
| **Professional Services** | -85% to -75% | Subtle, non-distracting |
| **Customer Support** | -75% to -60% | Present but background |
| **Sales** | -70% to -55% | Noticeable, creates energy |
| **Casual/Retail** | -65% to -50% | Clearly present, friendly vibe |
| **Healthcare** | -90% to -80% | Minimal, respectful |
| **Emergency Services** | -95% to -85% | Extremely subtle, focus on clarity |

### Adjusting Volume

**Process:**
1. Select ambience type
2. Click **Preview** to hear default volume
3. Adjust slider in small increments (5-10%)
4. Preview after each adjustment
5. Find sweet spot where ambience is present but not distracting
6. Save settings

**Testing tips:**
- Test in quiet environment with good headphones/speakers
- Listen to full conversation (not just samples)
- Have team members test and provide feedback
- Consider that customers may be in noisy environments (ambience less noticeable)

## Previewing and Testing Ambience

### Preview Function

**How to preview:**
1. Select ambience type from library
2. Adjust volume slider
3. Click **Preview** or **Play**
4. Sample plays with agent voice and ambience combined
5. Listen critically for balance

**What to listen for:**
- Can you hear the voice clearly?
- Is ambience noticeable but not distracting?
- Does it create the desired atmosphere?
- Are there any jarring or annoying elements?
- Does it feel natural after 30-60 seconds?

### Testing in Full Conversations

Preview samples are short. Test with real conversations:

1. Apply ambience settings to test agent
2. Start web call using Test Call feature
3. Have 3-5 minute conversation
4. Assess:
   - Does ambience become annoying over time?
   - Does it enhance or detract from experience?
   - Is voice still clear and understandable?
   - Does atmosphere match brand?

### A/B Testing Ambience

To scientifically compare options:

1. Create two identical test agents
2. Agent A: Ambience type 1 at volume X
3. Agent B: Ambience type 2 at volume Y
4. Have team members test both (blind test if possible)
5. Collect ratings:
   - Naturalness
   - Professionalism
   - Clarity
   - Overall preference
6. Deploy winner to production agent

## Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Start Subtle, Increase if Needed" icon="volume-low">
    Begin with very low volume (-85% to -75%):
    - Easier to increase than decrease
    - Less risk of overwhelming customers
    - Maintains clarity while testing

    Increase only if feedback suggests ambience isn't noticeable enough.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Match Ambience to Brand and Context" icon="palette">
    Choose ambience that aligns with your brand identity:

    **Professional/Corporate:** Office or Quiet Room
    **Friendly/Casual:** Café
    **Active/Energetic:** Call Center (moderate volume)
    **Serious/Sensitive:** Quiet Room or None

    Mismatched ambience (e.g., café for legal advice) can undermine credibility.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Test with Customer Noise in Mind" icon="phone">
    Customers may call from noisy environments:
    - Driving in car (road noise)
    - Public spaces (background conversations)
    - Outdoors (wind, traffic)

    Your subtle ambience may be completely inaudible to them. That's okay—it benefits customers in quiet environments without harming noisy ones.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Avoid Distracting Elements" icon="ban">
    Good ambience is consistent and non-intrusive:
    - **Avoid:** Sudden loud sounds (phone ringing at full volume)
    - **Avoid:** Music with lyrics or recognizable melodies
    - **Avoid:** Repetitive patterns (same sound looping obviously)
    - **Prefer:** Consistent, low-level background texture

    If customers comment on background sound, it's probably too loud or too distinct.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Consider Accessibility" icon="universal-access">
    Some customers may have hearing difficulties:
    - Keep ambience subtle to maximize voice clarity
    - Offer to disable ambience if customer requests
    - Test with hearing aid users if possible
    - Monitor accessibility feedback

    Clarity always trumps atmosphere.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Monitor Customer Feedback" icon="comments">
    After deploying ambience:
    - Review satisfaction scores (watch for drops)
    - Check for comments about background noise
    - Monitor comprehension and completion rates
    - Ask team members who listen to calls

    If metrics drop, reduce or remove ambience.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Common Ambience Scenarios

### Scenario 1: First-Time Setup

**Goal:** Add subtle ambience to make agent feel more natural

**Recommended approach:**
1. Choose **Quiet Room** or **Office** ambience
2. Set volume to -80% (very subtle)
3. Test with team members
4. Ask: "Do you notice background sound?" and "Does it enhance the experience?"
5. Adjust based on feedback (usually increase slightly to -70% or -75%)
6. Deploy to production
7. Monitor for 1-2 weeks before making further changes

### Scenario 2: Call Center Realism

**Goal:** Create impression of active, capable support team

**Recommended approach:**
1. Choose **Call Center** ambience
2. Start at -70% (subtle but noticeable)
3. Test with realistic scenarios
4. Verify it creates "busy but organized" feeling, not "chaos"
5. Consider lowering to -75% or -80% if too intense
6. Especially important: Test how it sounds during hold/transfer scenarios

### Scenario 3: Brand Differentiation

**Goal:** Stand out from competitors with unique atmosphere

**Recommended approach:**
1. Identify brand personality (professional? friendly? creative?)
2. Choose matching ambience:
   - Creative agency → Café
   - Law firm → Quiet Room
   - Logistics company → Street/Outdoor
3. Set volume moderate (-65% to -55%) so it's clearly present
4. Test that it reinforces brand without distracting
5. Use consistently across all agents for brand recognition

### Scenario 4: Removing Ambience

**Goal:** Simplify agent or respond to negative feedback

**Recommended approach:**
1. Gradually reduce volume over time:
   - Week 1: Reduce from -60% to -75%
   - Week 2: Reduce from -75% to -90%
   - Week 3: Disable entirely
2. Monitor metrics at each step
3. Identify if ambience was helping or harming
4. Restore to optimal level if metrics drop

<Note>
Sudden changes may affect customer experience. Gradual adjustments allow customers to adapt and give clearer data.
</Note>

## Backchannel (Coming Soon)

### What is Backchannel?

Backchannel refers to verbal and non-verbal responses that show active listening without interrupting:

**Examples:**
- "Mm-hmm" (understanding)
- "I see" (acknowledgment)
- "Okay" (confirmation)
- "Got it" (receipt of information)
- Brief affirmations while customer speaks

**Purpose:**
- Show agent is actively listening
- Encourage customer to continue
- Reduce dead silence during pauses
- Create more natural, human-like interaction

## Troubleshooting Ambience Issues

### Ambience Too Loud

**Symptoms:** Customers ask about background noise, complain it's hard to hear, or mention "busy environment"

**Solutions:**
- Reduce volume by 10-15% increments
- Switch to quieter ambience type (Call Center → Office → Quiet Room)
- Test with actual customers, not just team members
- Consider disabling ambience entirely

### Ambience Not Noticeable

**Symptoms:** Team members can't hear any background sound, no atmosphere created

**Solutions:**
- Increase volume by 10-15% increments
- Switch to more prominent ambience type
- Test with better audio equipment (good headphones)
- Verify ambience is actually enabled (not at -100%)

### Ambience Sounds Repetitive or Unnatural

**Symptoms:** Obvious loop points, same sound repeating, artificial feeling

**Solutions:**
- Test different ambience type (some have longer, more varied loops)
- Reduce volume (making it less noticeable may help)
- Report issue to support (ambience library may need update)

### Ambience Causes Comprehension Issues

**Symptoms:** Customers frequently ask for repetition, misunderstand agent, seem confused

**Solutions:**
- **Immediately reduce volume** (clarity is paramount)
- Test if issue is actually ambience or voice quality/speed
- Consider disabling ambience for this use case
- Ensure voice is clearly audible above ambience

### Technical Issues

**Symptoms:** Ambience not playing, cutting out, distorted, or volume control not working

**Solutions:**
- Refresh dashboard and try again
- Test with new conversation (changes may not apply to active calls)
- Verify agent is deployed/published
- Contact support if issue persists
