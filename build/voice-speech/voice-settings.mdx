## Overview

After selecting your agent's voice, you can fine-tune how it sounds through advanced voice settings. Adjustments to speed, pitch, and timbre help you achieve the perfect tone and pacing for your brand and use case.

<Note>
Access voice settings by clicking **Configure** next to your selected voice in the Voice tab. Changes apply immediately to new conversations.
</Note>

### Accessing Voice Settings

1. Navigate to **Voice** tab in agent configuration
2. Select your desired voice (or keep current selection)
3. Click **Configure** or **Settings** button next to the voice
4. Voice configuration panel opens with all available parameters

## Universal Voice Parameters

These parameters are available for most voice providers and have similar effects across different voices.

### Speed (Rate)

Controls how fast the voice speaks (range typically: 0.5x to 2.0x)

**Speed Values:**
- **0.5x - 0.7x:** Very slow
  - Use for: Elderly audiences, complex information, legal disclosures
  - Risk: May sound condescending or robotic

- **0.8x - 0.9x:** Slightly slow
  - Use for: Clearer enunciation, accented audiences, technical instructions
  - Benefit: More time for comprehension without sounding unnatural

- **1.0x:** Normal speed (default)
  - Use for: Most conversational agents
  - Standard: Matches natural human speech pace

- **1.1x - 1.2x:** Slightly fast
  - Use for: Energetic brands, younger audiences, time-sensitive calls
  - Benefit: Conveys efficiency and energy

- **1.3x - 2.0x:** Very fast
  - Use for: Rarely (sounds rushed and unnatural)
  - Risk: Customers may miss information or feel rushed

<Tip>
**Recommended starting point:** 1.0x for most agents. Adjust by 0.05-0.1x increments based on testing feedback. Small changes make a significant difference.
</Tip>

**Testing speed:**
- Test with your longest typical responses (30-60 seconds)
- Have team members listen to full conversations, not just samples
- Consider customer demographics (older customers often prefer slightly slower)
- Check comprehension: Can listeners understand and retain information?

### Pitch

Controls the fundamental frequency of the voice (range typically: -20% to +20%)

**Pitch Values:**
- **-20% to -10%:** Lower pitch
  - Effect: More authoritative, serious, masculine
  - Use for: Professional services, financial, legal, security
  - Risk: May sound monotone or robotic at extremes

- **-5% to +5%:** Slight adjustment
  - Effect: Subtle tonal shifts
  - Use for: Fine-tuning to match brand personality
  - Safe: Minimal risk of sounding unnatural

- **0%:** Default pitch (recommended)
  - Effect: Voice as trained/designed
  - Use for: Most applications

- **+10% to +20%:** Higher pitch
  - Effect: More energetic, youthful, feminine
  - Use for: Retail, hospitality, children's services
  - Risk: May sound artificial or squeaky at extremes

<Warning>
Large pitch adjustments (>15%) can make voices sound unnatural or robotic. Use sparingly and test thoroughly.
</Warning>

**Pitch considerations:**
- Higher pitch conveys energy but may reduce authority
- Lower pitch conveys authority but may reduce approachability
- Gender: Pitch doesn't change gender perception significantly
- Cultural factors: Pitch preferences vary by region and context

### Timbre (Tone Quality)

Some providers offer timbre or tone controls that affect the voice's character beyond pitch and speed.

**What timbre controls:**
- Warmth vs. brightness of the voice
- Resonance and richness
- Breathiness or clarity
- Overall tonal quality

**Timbre settings (provider-dependent):**
- **Warmer:** Fuller, softer, more intimate sound
- **Brighter:** Clearer, sharper, more energetic sound
- **Neutral:** Balanced tone (default)

Not all providers expose timbre controls. ElevenLabs and some Azure voices offer this parameter.

## Provider-Specific Parameters

### ElevenLabs Parameters

ElevenLabs voices offer unique parameters for controlling naturalness and consistency.

#### Stability

Controls variability and expressiveness (range: 0-100)

**How stability works:**
- **Low stability (0-40):** More variation between takes
  - Each generation of the same text sounds different
  - More expressive and emotional
  - Less consistent across multiple calls

- **Medium stability (50-60):** Balanced (recommended)
  - Some natural variation
  - Reasonably consistent
  - Maintains expressiveness

- **High stability (70-100):** Maximum consistency
  - Same text generates nearly identical audio every time
  - More robotic, less variation
  - Predictable output

**Recommended values:**
- **Customer service:** 60-70 (consistent but not robotic)
- **Creative/Marketing:** 30-50 (expressive, varied)
- **Compliance/Legal:** 80-90 (highly consistent, repeatable)

<Note>
Higher stability reduces the model's creative expressiveness but ensures the same sentence sounds identical across different calls—useful for regulated content.
</Note>

#### Similarity Boost

Controls how closely the voice matches the original speaker (range: 0-100)

**How similarity boost works:**
- **Low boost (0-40):** More creative interpretation
  - Voice may deviate from original character
  - Can add variety but may sound less "true to the voice"

- **Medium boost (50-70):** Balanced adherence
  - Maintains voice identity
  - Allows some natural variation

- **High boost (75-100):** Strict voice matching
  - Voice stays very close to original training
  - Reduces creativity and variation
  - Best for cloned/custom voices

**Recommended values:**
- **Standard ElevenLabs voices:** 60-75
- **Custom cloned voices:** 80-90 (to preserve identity)
- **Generic applications:** 50-60

#### Style Exaggeration

Some ElevenLabs models support style exaggeration for emotional range.

- **0%:** Neutral delivery
- **50%:** Moderate emotional expression
- **100%:** Maximum emotional range

Use higher values for customer service agents requiring empathy; lower for factual, information-only agents.

### Azure Speech Parameters

Azure voices support SSML (Speech Synthesis Markup Language) for fine-grained control.

#### Speaking Style

Azure Neural voices offer predefined speaking styles:

**Common styles:**
- `chat` - Casual, conversational tone
- `customerservice` - Friendly and helpful
- `newscast` - Formal, news anchor style
- `assistant` - Warm, helpful assistant tone
- `cheerful` - Upbeat and positive
- `sad` - Empathetic, somber
- `angry` - Firm and assertive (use carefully)
- `fearful` - Hesitant, nervous

**Setting styles:**
Configure via SSML in voice settings or dynamically in responses using Jinja templates.

Example SSML:
```xml
<speak version="1.0" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2001/10/synthesis"
       xmlns:mstts="https://www.w3.org/2001/mstts" xml:lang="en-US">
  <voice name="en-US-JennyNeural">
    <mstts:express-as style="customerservice">
      I'd be happy to help you with that today!
    </mstts:express-as>
  </voice>
</speak>
```

#### Role

Some Azure voices support role-playing:
- `YoungAdultFemale`
- `YoungAdultMale`
- `OlderAdultFemale`
- `OlderAdultMale`
- `SeniorFemale`
- `SeniorMale`

Useful for matching agent persona to customer demographics.

#### Prosody Controls

Azure SSML allows detailed prosody control:
- **Contour:** Pitch pattern across utterance
- **Range:** How much pitch varies
- **Rate:** Speed of speech
- **Volume:** Loudness

These can be applied to specific words or phrases within responses.

<Warning>
SSML requires proper formatting and escaping. Invalid SSML may cause voice synthesis to fail. Test thoroughly when using SSML.
</Warning>

## Advanced Voice Tuning

### Emphasis and Breaks

Add emphasis or pauses for clarity and impact.

#### Word/Phrase Emphasis

Some providers support emphasizing specific words:

**ElevenLabs:** Inherently adds natural emphasis
**Azure SSML:** Use `<emphasis>` tag
```xml
<emphasis level="strong">This is very important</emphasis>
```

**Use cases:**
- Highlighting key information: "Your balance is <emphasis>zero dollars</emphasis>"
- Adding urgency: "<emphasis>Immediately</emphasis> update your password"
- Clarifying: "I said <emphasis>fifteen</emphasis>, not fifty"

#### Breaks and Pauses

Insert pauses for pacing and comprehension:

**Azure SSML:**
```xml
<break time="500ms"/>  <!-- Half-second pause -->
<break time="1s"/>     <!-- One-second pause -->
<break strength="weak"/> <!-- Natural short pause -->
```

**Use cases:**
- After important information: "Your confirmation number is A-B-C-1-2-3 <break time="500ms"/> Did you get that?"
- Lists: "First <break strength="weak"/> second <break strength="weak"/> and third"
- Dramatic effect: "The answer is <break time="1s"/> yes!"

### Pronunciation Adjustments

For voices that don't pronounce words correctly:

**Option 1: Phonetic spelling**
Change text to phonetically spell the word:
- "itellicoAI" → "eye-tell-ee-co A I"
- "SQL" → "sequel" or "S Q L"

**Option 2: Custom pronunciations** (covered in detail in Custom Pronunciations page)
Use dedicated pronunciation features (pipeline agents only).

**Option 3: SSML phonemes** (Azure)
Use International Phonetic Alphabet:
```xml
<phoneme alphabet="ipa" ph="ˈaɪ.tɛl.iː.koʊ">itellicoAI</phoneme>
```

<Tip>
Simple phonetic spelling is easiest and works across all providers. Reserve IPA/SSML for complex pronunciations.
</Tip>

## JSON Payload and API Usage

### Viewing the JSON Configuration

The voice configuration interface displays the resulting JSON payload:

```json
{
  "provider": "elevenlabs",
  "voice_id": "21m00Tcm4TlvDq8ikWAM",
  "model": "eleven_monolingual_v1",
  "voice_settings": {
    "stability": 0.65,
    "similarity_boost": 0.75,
    "style": 0.0,
    "use_speaker_boost": true
  },
  "speed": 1.05,
  "optimize_streaming_latency": 3
}
```

### Copying Configuration

Use the JSON payload to:
- **Document voice settings** for your records
- **Replicate settings** across multiple agents
- **API integration** if building custom interfaces
- **Version control** track voice setting changes over time

**How to copy:**
1. Open voice configuration panel
2. Adjust settings as desired
3. Click **View JSON** or **Copy Configuration**
4. Paste into your documentation or code

### Applying JSON Configurations

To apply a saved configuration:
1. Open voice configuration panel
2. Click **Import from JSON** (if available)
3. Paste your JSON configuration
4. Verify settings loaded correctly
5. Save

Alternatively, configure manually based on saved JSON values.

## Testing Voice Settings

### Systematic Testing Process

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="1. Baseline Test" icon="play">
    **Before making changes:**
    - Test current voice with default settings
    - Run through 3-5 realistic conversation scenarios
    - Record or take notes on what sounds good/bad
    - Identify specific issues to address:
      - "Too fast for older customers"
      - "Not authoritative enough"
      - "Sounds monotone"
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="2. Incremental Adjustments" icon="sliders">
    **Make one change at a time:**
    - Adjust only one parameter (e.g., speed)
    - Change in small increments (0.05-0.1 for speed, 5-10 for stability)
    - Test after each change
    - Note the effect before making another adjustment

    **Why?** Changing multiple parameters simultaneously makes it impossible to identify which change improved/worsened the voice.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="3. A/B Comparison" icon="code-compare">
    **Compare settings directly:**
    - Create two test agents with different voice settings
    - Use identical instructions and scenarios
    - Have team members test both without knowing which is which
    - Collect ratings and preferences
    - Choose winner or iterate further

    **Example:**
    - Agent A: Speed 1.0x, Stability 60
    - Agent B: Speed 1.1x, Stability 70
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="4. Long-Form Testing" icon="clock">
    **Test extended conversations:**
    - Have a 5-10 minute conversation with the agent
    - Longer interactions reveal issues short samples hide:
      - Voice fatigue (repetitive, annoying after time)
      - Pacing problems (too fast/slow sustained)
      - Consistency issues

    **Question to ask:** Would I be comfortable listening to this for 10 minutes?
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="5. Diverse Speaker Testing" icon="users">
    **Get multiple perspectives:**
    - Different ages (voice preferences vary by age)
    - Different roles (customer service team, executives, customers)
    - Different contexts (quiet office vs. noisy environment)

    Collect feedback systematically using a rating form.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Evaluation Criteria

Rate voice settings on these dimensions (1-5 scale):

| Criteria | What to Assess |
|----------|---------------|
| **Clarity** | Can you understand every word easily? |
| **Naturalness** | Does it sound like a real person? |
| **Pacing** | Is the speed comfortable? |
| **Brand fit** | Does it match your brand personality? |
| **Pleasantness** | Is it pleasant to listen to for several minutes? |
| **Authority** | Does it convey appropriate expertise? |
| **Warmth** | Does it sound friendly and approachable? |
| **Energy** | Does it match the desired energy level? |

Aim for 4+ on all criteria relevant to your use case.

## Common Voice Settings by Use Case

### Customer Support

**Objective:** Helpful, patient, professional

**Recommended settings:**
- Speed: 0.95x - 1.0x (slightly slower for clarity)
- Pitch: 0% to +5% (slightly higher for approachability)
- Stability (ElevenLabs): 65-70 (consistent but warm)
- Similarity Boost: 70-75
- Style: Warm, empathetic

**Why:**
- Slower pace ensures customers understand solutions
- Slight higher pitch conveys friendliness
- High stability ensures consistent quality answers

### Sales and Lead Qualification

**Objective:** Confident, energetic, persuasive

**Recommended settings:**
- Speed: 1.05x - 1.1x (energetic pace)
- Pitch: -5% to 0% (authoritative)
- Stability (ElevenLabs): 55-65 (expressive)
- Similarity Boost: 60-70
- Style: Enthusiastic, confident

**Why:**
- Faster pace conveys energy and momentum
- Neutral/lower pitch conveys confidence
- Lower stability allows expressive variation

### Healthcare and Medical

**Objective:** Professional, empathetic, clear

**Recommended settings:**
- Speed: 0.9x - 0.95x (clear, unhurried)
- Pitch: 0% (neutral, professional)
- Stability (ElevenLabs): 70-75 (very consistent)
- Similarity Boost: 75-80
- Style: Calm, reassuring, professional

**Why:**
- Slower pace critical for medical information comprehension
- Neutral pitch maintains professionalism
- High stability ensures repeatable delivery of medical info

### Technical Support

**Objective:** Patient, knowledgeable, clear

**Recommended settings:**
- Speed: 0.9x - 0.95x (clear for instructions)
- Pitch: -5% to 0% (knowledgeable)
- Stability (ElevenLabs): 65-70
- Similarity Boost: 70-75
- Style: Patient, instructional

**Why:**
- Slower pace essential for technical instructions
- Slightly lower pitch conveys expertise
- Moderate stability balances consistency and warmth

### Appointment Booking

**Objective:** Efficient, friendly, organized

**Recommended settings:**
- Speed: 1.0x - 1.05x (efficient)
- Pitch: 0% to +5% (friendly)
- Stability (ElevenLabs): 70-75 (consistent)
- Similarity Boost: 70-75
- Style: Professional, helpful

**Why:**
- Standard to slightly fast pace maintains efficiency
- Slight higher pitch for friendliness
- High stability for repeatable appointment confirmations

### Collections and Billing

**Objective:** Professional, firm but respectful

**Recommended settings:**
- Speed: 0.95x - 1.0x (measured)
- Pitch: -5% to -10% (authoritative)
- Stability (ElevenLabs): 75-80 (very consistent)

- Standard pace maintains professionalism

## Troubleshooting Voice Issues

### Voice Sounds Robotic

**Symptoms:** Unnatural, mechanical, lacking expression

**Possible causes:**
1. Too high stability (ElevenLabs)
2. Extreme pitch or speed adjustments
3. Poor quality voice model
4. Choppy audio from network issues

**Solutions:**
- Lower stability to 50-60 (ElevenLabs)
- Reset speed and pitch to defaults
- Try a different, more natural voice (e.g., ElevenLabs premium)
- Check network latency and audio quality

### Voice Too Fast/Slow

**Symptoms:** Customers miss information or feel rushed/bored

**Solutions:**
- Adjust speed in 0.05x increments
- Test with real conversations (not just samples)
- Consider customer demographics (older = slower)
- Check if instructions cause repetitive, lengthy responses

### Pronunciation Errors

**Symptoms:** Voice mispronounces brand names, technical terms, acronyms

**Solutions:**
- Use phonetic spelling in instructions/responses
- Add to custom pronunciations (pipeline agents)
- Use SSML phonemes (Azure)
- Test different voices (some pronounce certain words better)

See Custom Pronunciations page for detailed solutions.

### Voice Quality Varies

**Symptoms:** Sometimes clear, sometimes muffled or distorted

**Possible causes:**
1. Network quality fluctuations
2. Low stability setting causing variation
3. Provider service issues
4. Audio codec problems

**Solutions:**
- Check network stability and latency
- Increase stability setting (ElevenLabs)
- Test at different times to rule out provider issues
- Contact support if persistent

### Voice Lacks Energy/Emotion

**Symptoms:** Monotone, flat, disengaged

**Solutions:**
- Lower stability for more expressiveness (ElevenLabs)
- Adjust style/emotion settings (Azure speaking style)
- Choose more expressive voice model
- Write more dynamic instructions (varied phrasing, shorter sentences)

### Voice Too Harsh/Aggressive

**Symptoms:** Sounds unfriendly, impatient, or rude

**Solutions:**
- Increase pitch slightly (+5% to +10%)
- Slow down speed (0.9x - 0.95x)
- Choose warmer voice model
- Adjust instructions to use gentler language

## Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Start with Defaults" icon="rotate-left">
    Always begin testing with default voice settings (1.0x speed, 0% pitch, default stability):
    - Defaults are optimized by voice designers
    - Easier to identify what needs adjustment
    - Avoids compounding adjustments

    Make changes only when you identify specific issues.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Make Small Adjustments" icon="gauge">
    Voice settings are sensitive—small changes have big impacts:
    - Speed: Adjust by 0.05x - 0.1x at a time
    - Pitch: Adjust by 5% increments
    - Stability: Adjust by 5-10 points

    Test after each small change before making another.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Test in Context" icon="comments">
    Don't optimize based on single-sentence samples:
    - Test full conversations (3-5 minutes minimum)
    - Use realistic scenarios from your use case
    - Include complex responses (explanations, instructions)
    - Test edge cases (numbers, spelling, addresses)

    What sounds good in a sample may not work in real calls.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Get Diverse Feedback" icon="users">
    Your preferences may not match customer preferences:
    - Test with team members of different ages and backgrounds
    - Consider target customer demographics
    - Run A/B tests with small customer segments if possible
    - Measure satisfaction and comprehension, not just preference

    Data beats opinions.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Document Your Settings" icon="file-lines">
    Keep records of voice configurations:
    - Copy JSON payload to your documentation
    - Note the rationale for settings ("Slower for elderly customers")
    - Track changes over time
    - Document what worked and what didn't

    This helps when replicating settings or training new team members.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Retest After Provider Updates" icon="rotate">
    Voice providers update their models periodically:
    - Improved voices may sound different
    - Your optimized settings may need adjustment
    - Test after receiving update notifications
    - Re-baseline if voice character changes significantly

    Subscribe to provider changelogs and itellicoAI update emails.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
