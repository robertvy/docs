## Overview

After selecting your voice, you can adjust provider-specific settings to fine-tune how it sounds. Available settings depend on which voice provider you've selected.

<Note>
Voice settings are displayed dynamically based on your selected voice. Changes apply immediately to new conversations.
</Note>

---

## ElevenLabs Settings

ElevenLabs voices support the following adjustable parameters:

### Stability

Controls consistency and expressiveness (range: 0.0-1.0, default: 0.71)

**How it works:**
- **Lower values (0.3-0.5):** More expressive and varied, but less consistent between generations
- **Medium values (0.5-0.7):** Balanced expressiveness and consistency (recommended)
- **Higher values (0.7-1.0):** More consistent and predictable, but may sound monotone

**Recommended starting point:** 0.71 (default)

Use lower stability for creative applications where variety is desired, and higher stability for consistent customer service responses.

### Similarity Boost

Controls how closely the voice matches the original speaker (range: 0.0-1.0, default: 0.75)

**How it works:**
- **Lower values (0.5-0.7):** More creative interpretation of the voice
- **Medium values (0.7-0.8):** Balanced adherence to original voice (recommended)
- **Higher values (0.8-1.0):** Strict matching to original voice character

**Recommended starting point:** 0.75 (default)

Use higher similarity boost for custom/cloned voices where preserving the original character is critical.

### Style

Controls stylistic variation in pacing and intonation (range: 0.0-1.0, default: 0.0)

**How it works:**
- **0.0:** Neutral delivery (recommended)
- **0.5-1.0:** Amplified style of the original speaker

**Recommended starting point:** 0.0 (default)

Higher style values can make voices less stable. It's generally recommended to keep this at 0.

### Speaker Boost

Enhances clarity and presence (boolean, default: true)

**How it works:**
- **Enabled:** Boosts similarity to the original speaker, improving clarity
- **Disabled:** Standard processing

**Recommended starting point:** Enabled (default)

Adds minimal latency but improves voice quality.

### Speed

Controls playback speed (range: 0.7-1.2, default: 1.0)

**Speed values:**
- **0.7-0.9:** Slower, clearer delivery
- **1.0:** Normal speed (default)
- **1.1-1.2:** Faster, more energetic delivery

**Recommended starting point:** 1.0 (default)

Adjust in small increments (0.05-0.1) and test with full conversations.

## Cartesia Settings

Cartesia voices support the following adjustable parameter:

### Speech Rate

Controls how fast the voice speaks (range: 0.5-2.0, default: 1.0)

**Speech rate values:**
- **0.5-0.8:** Slower delivery for clarity
- **1.0:** Normal speed (default)
- **1.2-2.0:** Faster delivery for efficiency

**Recommended starting point:** 1.0 (default)

Cartesia's ultra-low latency makes speed adjustments feel responsive. Test with realistic conversation scenarios.

## Azure Speech Settings

Azure Speech voices do not currently support adjustable settings through the itellico AI interface.

Azure uses default voice configurations optimized by Microsoft for each neural voice. For advanced customization, Azure supports SSML (Speech Synthesis Markup Language), which can be used in your agent's response templates.

<Note>
If you need advanced Azure voice customization (speaking styles, prosody, emphasis), contact support for guidance on implementing SSML in your agent responses.
</Note>

## Adjusting Settings

### How to Change Voice Settings

1. Navigate to **Voice** tab in your agent configuration
2. Your currently selected voice is displayed at the top
3. Voice settings appear below based on your voice provider
4. Adjust sliders or toggles as needed
5. Settings save automatically

### Testing Your Changes

After adjusting voice settings:

1. Click **Test** to hear a preview with the new settings
2. Make a test call to your agent to hear the voice in a real conversation
3. Adjust further if needed based on how it sounds in context

**Important:** Test voice settings with full conversations, not just single sentences. What sounds good in a preview may not work well in extended interactions.

## Common Settings by Use Case

### Customer Support
- **ElevenLabs:** Stability 0.65-0.75, Similarity 0.75, Style 0.0, Speed 1.0
- **Cartesia:** Speech Rate 1.0
- **Goal:** Consistent, clear, professional

### Sales
- **ElevenLabs:** Stability 0.60-0.70, Similarity 0.70, Style 0.0, Speed 1.05-1.1
- **Cartesia:** Speech Rate 1.1-1.2
- **Goal:** Energetic, confident, engaging

### Technical Support
- **ElevenLabs:** Stability 0.70-0.75, Similarity 0.75, Style 0.0, Speed 0.95
- **Cartesia:** Speech Rate 0.9-0.95
- **Goal:** Clear, patient, instructional

### Healthcare
- **ElevenLabs:** Stability 0.75-0.80, Similarity 0.80, Style 0.0, Speed 0.9-0.95
- **Cartesia:** Speech Rate 0.9
- **Goal:** Professional, empathetic, clear

## Best Practices

**Start with defaults:** Default settings are optimized by voice providers. Only adjust when you identify specific issues.

**Make small changes:** Voice settings are sensitive. Adjust in small increments and test after each change.

**Test in context:** Use full conversation scenarios (3-5 minutes), not just single-sentence samples.

**Consider your audience:** Older customers often prefer slightly slower speeds. Younger audiences may prefer slightly faster.

**Document your settings:** Keep track of what works for each use case and voice combination.
