# Preamble

For the purpose of evaluating a technical partnership regarding the integration of the Discloser's Voice-AI platform into the Recipient's infrastructure, enabling the Recipient to offer Voice-AI solutions to its customers (the "Purpose"), the Discloser may disclose Confidential Information (as defined below) to the Recipient. The Recipient agrees to strict confidentiality in accordance with this Agreement. This Agreement becomes effective on the date of the last signature.

## 1. Definition of Confidential Information

### 1.1 Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" includes all technical, business, or other information of the Discloser, particularly software (including source code and algorithms), software architecture, database designs, API specifications and documentation, frontend and backend designs, user interface concepts (UI/UX designs), development methods and processes, test data and protocols, security concepts, technical specifications and diagrams, information about technologies and frameworks used, trade secrets, products, services, customer lists and data, business plans, marketing strategies, financial data, price lists, enterprise pricing structures, individual customer agreements, and all other technical or business information disclosed to the Recipient by the Discloser in writing, electronically, or otherwise, directly or indirectly. This also includes information disclosed prior to the effective date of this Agreement as well as the existence and content of this Agreement.

### 1.2 Exceptions to Confidential Information

Information is not considered Confidential Information if the Recipient can prove that it:

- was publicly known and generally accessible prior to disclosure by the Discloser;
- becomes publicly known and generally accessible after disclosure by the Discloser to the Recipient without any unlawful act or omission by the Recipient;
- was already lawfully in the possession of the Recipient at the time of disclosure by the Discloser, as evidenced by written records and documentation of the Recipient immediately prior to the time of disclosure;
- was lawfully obtained by the Recipient from a third party without breach of confidentiality obligations of that third party to the Discloser or any other party;
- was independently developed by the Recipient without use or reference to the Discloser's Confidential Information, as evidenced by documents and other appropriate evidence in the possession of the Recipient; or
- is required to be disclosed by law, court order, subpoena, or governmental regulation, provided that the Recipient gives the Discloser prompt written notice of such requirement and cooperates with the Discloser's efforts to seek protective orders or similar relief.

### 1.3 Personal Data and GDPR Compliance

**Personal Data Exclusion:** Personal data of customers, end-users, or other data subjects as defined under Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR) is expressly excluded from the definition of "Confidential Information" under this Agreement. Such personal data shall be governed exclusively by:

- The applicable Data Processing Agreement (DPA) between the parties;
- GDPR and other applicable data protection laws;
- Specific data processing instructions provided by the data controller.

**Data Subject Rights:** Nothing in this Agreement shall prevent or restrict either party from:

- Fulfilling obligations under GDPR, including responding to data subject requests (access, rectification, erasure, portability, restriction of processing);
- Complying with lawful requests from data protection authorities;
- Implementing data breach notification requirements;
- Conducting Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA) where required.

**Precedence:** In case of any conflict between the confidentiality obligations under this Agreement and obligations under applicable data protection laws or the DPA, the data protection obligations shall take precedence.

**Separate Agreements:** The parties acknowledge that personal data processing activities may be governed by separate agreements (such as a Data Processing Agreement) that contain specific provisions for data protection compliance, data retention periods, and technical and organizational measures.

### 1.4 AI/ML Specific Confidential Information

For the purposes of this Agreement, Confidential Information specifically includes, but is not limited to:

**AI/ML Systems:** Machine learning models, neural network architectures, training datasets, validation datasets, model parameters, hyperparameters, optimization algorithms, feature engineering techniques, data preprocessing methods, and performance metrics.

**Voice-AI Specific:** Speech recognition algorithms, natural language processing models, voice synthesis parameters, acoustic models, language models, pronunciation dictionaries, voice biometric data, conversation flow designs, and dialogue management systems.

**Training and Operational Data:** Training methodologies, model training logs, inference patterns, usage analytics, performance benchmarks, A/B testing results, and operational metrics that could reveal system capabilities or limitations.

**Integration Specifications:** API rate limits, system capacity information, scalability parameters, failover mechanisms, and integration patterns that could reveal technical capabilities or competitive advantages.

## 2. Confidentiality and Non-Use

### 2.1 Obligation of Confidentiality

The Recipient shall keep all Confidential Information strictly confidential and use it only for the Purpose. It shall only be made accessible to agents who need it for the Purpose and who are demonstrably subject to at least equivalent confidentiality obligations. The Recipient is liable for its agents as for its own fault and shall provide evidence of the agents' obligations upon request by the Discloser.

### 2.2 Protection of Confidential Information

The Recipient shall protect Confidential Information with at least the same care it applies to its own highly confidential information, but at least with objectively reasonable care to prevent disclosure and unauthorized use.

### 2.3 Prohibition of Reverse Engineering

The Recipient is prohibited from reverse engineering, disassembling, decompiling, deriving the structure, composition, or source code of Confidential Information, or intensively analyzing or testing the operation of API endpoints (application programming interfaces) to infer the underlying source code, system architecture, business logic, or undocumented functionalities beyond the explicitly defined scope of use for the Purpose. It is also prohibited, without prior written consent of the Discloser, to use, exploit, or make Confidential Information the subject of commercial protection rights applications or claims, either by itself or through third parties.

## 3. Ownership and Return of Confidential Information

### 3.1 Ownership of Materials

All Confidential Information and its embodiments (e.g., documents, data carriers, copies, extracts) disclosed by the Discloser to the Recipient ("Materials") remain the exclusive property of the Discloser.

### 3.2 Return or Destruction of Materials

Upon request by the Discloser, and no later than upon achievement of the Purpose, the Recipient shall promptly return all Materials to the Discloser or, at the Discloser's option, demonstrably destroy them (including deletion of electronic storage). This also applies to copies or records made by the Recipient, which are only permitted to the extent absolutely necessary for the Purpose. Destruction/deletion must be confirmed in writing.

**Personal Data Exception:** The return or destruction obligations in this section do not apply to personal data, which shall be handled exclusively in accordance with the applicable DPA, GDPR requirements, and specific data retention obligations. Personal data retention and deletion shall be governed by the data retention periods and procedures specified in the DPA or as required by applicable data protection laws.

### 3.3 Waiver of Retention Rights

The Recipient waives any right of retention to the Materials. Statutory retention obligations of the Recipient remain unaffected; confidentiality continues to apply to such information.

## 4. Term

### 4.1 Duration of Obligations

The confidentiality and non-use obligations of this Agreement apply for the duration of the business relationship or discussions between the parties regarding a potential collaboration (whichever lasts longer) and for a period of three (3) years after their complete termination. The date of complete termination of the relationship or discussions is decisive for the start of this three-year period.

### 4.2 Protection of Trade Secrets

Notwithstanding Section 4.1, obligations to protect information that qualifies as trade secrets (e.g., under the Unfair Competition Act - UWG) shall continue as long as such information enjoys statutory protection as a trade secret.

## 5. No Obligation or License

This Agreement does not create any obligation to disclose information or to conduct further business, nor does it grant licenses to the Discloser's intellectual property, unless expressly agreed otherwise in writing.

## 6. No Warranty

All Confidential Information is provided "as is." The Discloser makes no express, implied, or other warranties regarding its completeness, accuracy, merchantability, fitness for a particular purpose, or non-infringement of third-party rights, unless such warranties are legally mandatory and cannot be excluded.

## 7. Remedies and Penalty

### 7.1 Injunctive Relief

In the event of a breach or threatened breach of this Agreement, the Discloser is entitled, in addition to other remedies, to seek injunctive relief without bond (to the extent permitted by law), as financial compensation is often insufficient.

### 7.2 Contractual Penalty

For each breach of Section 2, the Recipient agrees to pay a no-fault contractual penalty of €5,000 to the Discloser. Further claims by the Discloser (e.g., for damages, information, removal) remain unaffected; the contractual penalty is not credited against claims for damages.

### 7.3 Transfer of Rights

If the Recipient unlawfully uses Confidential Information to obtain or apply for commercial protection rights, it must immediately and gratuitously transfer all resulting rights and applications to the Discloser. This also applies to claims from exploitation agreements that the Recipient concludes in violation of this Agreement.

### 7.4 Liability for Damages

The Recipient is liable to the Discloser for all damages arising from violations of this Agreement by it, its agents, or third parties to whom it has unlawfully made Confidential Information accessible.

## 8. Security and International Considerations

### 8.1 Security Requirements

The Recipient shall implement and maintain appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect Confidential Information, including but not limited to:
- Access controls limiting access to authorized personnel only;
- Secure storage with encryption for electronic data;
- Network security measures for data transmission;
- Regular security audits and vulnerability assessments;
- Incident response procedures for security breaches;
- Employee training on confidentiality obligations.

**Breach Notification:** In the event of any actual or suspected security breach, unauthorized access, or disclosure of Confidential Information, the Recipient shall immediately (within 24 hours of discovery) notify the Discloser at [privacy@itellico.ai](mailto:privacy@itellico.ai) and provide a detailed incident report including the nature of the breach, affected information, corrective measures taken, and steps to prevent future occurrences.

### 8.2 Cross-Border Data Transfers

If Confidential Information is transferred across international borders or accessed from multiple jurisdictions, the Recipient shall:
- Ensure compliance with applicable data protection laws in all relevant jurisdictions;
- Implement appropriate safeguards for international transfers as required by law;
- Notify the Discloser of any cross-border transfers and obtain prior written consent where required;
- Comply with any export control laws or sanctions that may apply to the Confidential Information.

### 8.3 Third-Party Service Providers

If the Recipient engages third-party service providers who may have access to Confidential Information (including cloud service providers, IT support, or consultants), the Recipient shall:
- Ensure such third parties are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as restrictive as those in this Agreement;
- Remain fully liable for any breaches by such third parties;
- Provide the Discloser with a list of such third parties upon request;
- Obtain prior written consent from the Discloser for any high-risk third-party engagements.

## 9. Miscellaneous Provisions

### 9.1 Assignment

This Agreement binds successors and assigns. The Recipient may only transfer rights or obligations hereunder with the prior written consent of the Discloser. The Discloser may assign this Agreement to an affiliated company.

### 9.2 Governing Law; Jurisdiction

This Agreement is governed by Austrian law, excluding the UN Convention on Contracts for the International Sale of Goods and its conflict of laws rules. The exclusive place of jurisdiction for all disputes arising from or in connection with this Agreement is the court responsible for the Discloser's registered office in Vienna.

### 9.3 Entire Agreement

This Agreement constitutes the entire understanding of the parties and supersedes all prior oral or written agreements regarding the subject matter.

### 9.4 Waiver

A waiver of rights or provisions of this Agreement is only effective in writing. A one-time or delayed waiver does not constitute a waiver of future exercise.

### 9.5 Amendments

Amendments or supplements to this Agreement, as well as a departure from this written form requirement, require the written form and signature of both parties.

### 9.6 Severability Clause

Should any provisions of this Agreement be or become invalid or unenforceable, this shall not affect the validity of the remaining provisions. The parties shall replace the invalid/unenforceable provision with a valid one that comes as close as possible to the economic purpose of the original provision. The same applies to contractual gaps.

### 9.7 Notices

Notices under this Agreement must be in writing (email with confirmation of receipt or proof is sufficient) to the addresses mentioned at the beginning or another address communicated in writing.
