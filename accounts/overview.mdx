## Overview

itellicoAI uses a hierarchical account system that allows you to organize your teams, manage access control, and scale your voice AI operations. Whether you're a single team or an enterprise with multiple departments, the accounts system provides flexibility and security.

## Account Types

### Main Accounts

Main accounts (also called "root accounts" or "parent accounts") are top-level organizations with:

- Independent billing and subscriptions
- Full administrative control
- Ability to create unlimited subaccounts (subscription dependent)
- Separate API keys and integrations

**Use cases:**
- Individual companies
- Agencies managing multiple clients
- Enterprises with distinct business units

### Subaccounts

Subaccounts are nested under parent accounts and provide:

- Isolated workspace for teams or clients
- Separate agents, contacts, and conversations
- Independent team members and permissions
- Shared billing with parent account

**Key characteristics:**
- Unlimited nesting depth (subaccounts can have their own subaccounts)
- Parent accounts can access child data
- Children **cannot** access parent or sibling data
- Each subaccount has its own owner and team

**Use cases:**
- Agency client workspaces
- Department separation within enterprises
- Regional or product-line divisions
- Reseller customer accounts

## Account Hierarchy

The system follows a parent-child hierarchy with strict access control:

```
Parent Account (Agency)
├── Subaccount A (Client 1)
│   ├── Sub-subaccount A1 (Client 1 - Sales Team)
│   └── Sub-subaccount A2 (Client 1 - Support Team)
├── Subaccount B (Client 2)
└── Subaccount C (Client 3)
```

### Access Rules

- **Downward access**: Parents can access all child subaccounts
- **No upward access**: Children cannot access parent data
- **No sibling access**: Subaccounts cannot access each other
- **Context switching**: Users with multiple memberships can switch between accounts

## User Roles and Permissions

Each team member has one of four roles:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Owner" icon="crown">
    **Full administrative control** of the account.

    Permissions:
    - All permissions (wildcard access)
    - Manage team members and roles
    - Manage account settings and branding
    - Create and manage subaccounts
    - Manage billing and subscriptions
    - Delete the account
    - Cannot be removed by others

    Limitations:
    - Only **one owner** per account
    - Owner role cannot be transferred via UI (contact support)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Admin" icon="user-shield">
    **Administrative privileges** without billing access.

    Permissions:
    - Manage team members (add, remove, change roles)
    - Manage account settings
    - Create and manage subaccounts (if subscription allows)
    - Manage integrations and API keys
    - Full access to agents and conversations

    Limitations:
    - Cannot delete the account
    - Cannot remove the owner
    - Cannot access billing settings
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Member" icon="user">
    **Standard user** with full access to features.

    Permissions:
    - Create and edit agents
    - Access all conversations
    - Manage own content
    - Use test features
    - View team members

    Limitations:
    - Cannot manage team members
    - Cannot change account settings
    - Cannot create subaccounts
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Viewer" icon="eye">
    **Read-only access** to the account.

    Permissions:
    - View agents and configurations
    - View conversation logs
    - View team members

    Limitations:
    - Cannot create or edit anything
    - Cannot access settings
    - Cannot invite members
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Managing Accounts

### Creating a Main Account

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Account Switcher">
    Click your account name in the top navigation bar.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create New Account">
    Click **Create new account** and select **Main Account**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure Account">
    Enter the account name and confirm.

    You become the **Owner** of the new account automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Main accounts have separate billing. You'll need to configure a subscription for the new account.
</Note>

### Creating a Subaccount

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Subaccounts">
    Go to **Settings → Subaccounts** in your parent account.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create Subaccount">
    Click **Create Subaccount** and enter the name.

    **Requirements:**
    - You must be an Admin or Owner
    - Your subscription must include subaccount management
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure Team">
    You become the Owner of the subaccount. Invite team members as needed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Subaccounts inherit billing from the parent account. Usage is tracked separately but billed to the parent.
</Tip>

### Switching Between Accounts

If you're a member of multiple accounts:

1. Click your account name in the top navigation
2. Browse the account tree
3. Click any account to switch context
4. All data, agents, and settings update to reflect the selected account

<Note>
The selected account persists per browser tab, allowing you to work in multiple accounts simultaneously.
</Note>

## Team Member Management

### Inviting Members

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Team Settings">
    Navigate to **Settings → Team** in your account.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Send Invitation">
    Click **Invite Members** and enter email addresses.

    New members default to **Member** role.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Member Accepts">
    Invitees receive an email with a secure link to accept or decline.

    Invitations expire after 7 days.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
You can resend or cancel pending invitations from the team page.
</Tip>

### Changing Member Roles

**Admins and Owners** can change member roles:

1. Go to **Settings → Team**
2. Find the member in the list
3. Click the role dropdown
4. Select new role: Admin, Member, or Viewer

<Warning>
You cannot change the Owner role. Contact support to transfer ownership.
</Warning>

### Removing Members

**Only Owners** can remove team members:

1. Go to **Settings → Team**
2. Click the menu (⋯) next to a member
3. Select **Remove from team**
4. Confirm removal

<Note>
- You cannot remove yourself
- You cannot remove other Owners
- Removed members lose all access immediately
</Note>

## API Keys and Programmatic Access

API keys provide programmatic access to your account:

### Creating API Keys

1. Navigate to **Settings → API Keys**
2. Click **Create API Key**
3. Enter a label (e.g., "Production Server")
4. **Copy the key immediately** - it's shown only once
5. Store securely (treat like a password)

<Warning>
API keys have full access to your account. Never commit them to version control or share publicly.
</Warning>

### Key Format

```
sk-{8-char-prefix}.{55-random-chars}
```

- The prefix helps identify keys in logs
- Full key is hashed and cannot be retrieved

### Managing Keys

- **Revoke**: Disable a key without deleting (can reactivate later)
- **Delete**: Permanently remove a key
- **Set expiration**: Auto-expire keys on a specific date
- **Last used**: Track when each key was last accessed

<Tip>
Use separate API keys for different environments (development, staging, production) and rotate them regularly.
</Tip>

- **Account Name**: Display name throughout the platform
- **Account Status**: Active/Inactive (soft disable)

## Security Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Use the principle of least privilege" icon="shield-halved">
    - Assign Viewer role for read-only needs
    - Reserve Admin role for those managing the team
    - Limit Owner role to 1-2 people
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Organize with subaccounts" icon="sitemap">
    - Create separate subaccounts for clients
    - Use subaccounts to isolate departments
    - Leverage hierarchy for access control
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Manage API keys carefully" icon="key">
    - Create separate keys per environment
    - Revoke unused or compromised keys immediately
    - Set expiration dates for temporary access
    - Monitor last used timestamps
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Audit team members regularly" icon="clipboard-list">
    - Review team members quarterly
    - Remove departing employees immediately
    - Update roles when responsibilities change
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Common Use Cases

### Agency Managing Multiple Clients

```
Agency (Parent Account)
├── Client A Subaccount
│   └── Client A staff has full access
├── Client B Subaccount
│   └── Client B staff has full access
└── Agency retains visibility across all clients
```

**Benefits:**
- Clients can't see each other's data
- Agency admins can manage all accounts
- Separate billing tracking per client
- Easy onboarding and offboarding

### Enterprise with Regional Teams

```
Company HQ (Parent Account)
├── North America Region
│   ├── Sales Team
│   └── Support Team
├── Europe Region
│   ├── Sales Team
│   └── Support Team
└── APAC Region
```

**Benefits:**
- Regional autonomy with central oversight
- Department-level access control
- Consolidated billing and reporting
- Flexible team structures

### Reseller or White-Label Provider

```
Reseller (Parent Account)
├── Customer 1 (Subaccount)
├── Customer 2 (Subaccount)
└── Customer 3 (Subaccount)
```

**Benefits:**
- Customer isolation and privacy
- Centralized billing to reseller
- Custom branding per customer
- Scalable customer onboarding

## FAQs

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How many subaccounts can I create?">
    The number of subaccounts depends on your subscription plan. Contact sales to discuss your needs.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I transfer a subaccount to another parent?">
    Contact support to transfer subaccounts between parents or convert subaccounts to main accounts.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Who gets billed for subaccount usage?">
    The parent account is responsible for all subaccount billing. Usage is tracked separately for reporting.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can subaccounts have their own API keys?">
    Yes! Each subaccount can create independent API keys. Keys are scoped to their account context.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What happens to data when I deactivate an account?">
    Accounts are soft-deleted (marked inactive). Data is preserved and can be reactivated by contacting support.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can one user be a member of multiple accounts?">
    Yes! Users can be invited to multiple accounts and switch between them using the account switcher.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I transfer ownership of an account?">
    Contact support to transfer the Owner role. This cannot be done through the UI for security reasons.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
