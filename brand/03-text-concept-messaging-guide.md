## ✅ Writing Principles

### 1. Say What You Mean
**Principle:** Direct communication over marketing speak.

❌ **Don't:**
- "Leverage cutting-edge AI to revolutionize your customer experience"
- "Transform your business with next-generation voice technology"
- "Unlock the power of conversational AI"

✅ **Do:**
- "Automate 70% of routine calls while maintaining GDPR compliance"
- "Voice AI that answers questions, books appointments, and escalates complex cases to humans"
- "Production-ready in 5 days with 99.9% uptime guaranteed"

### 2. Specifics Over Superlatives
**Principle:** Prove it with numbers, not adjectives.

❌ **Don't:**
- "Amazing voice quality"
- "Lightning-fast response times"
- "World-class security"

✅ **Do:**
- "Natural-sounding German voices rated 8.7/10 for authenticity"
- "Average response latency: 1.2 seconds"
- "ISO 27001 certified, GDPR compliant, annual security audits"

### 3. German Precision Mindset
**Principle:** Be thorough, accurate, complete.

❌ **Don't:**
- "We handle compliance"
- "Easy integration"
- "Great support"

✅ **Do:**
- "GDPR compliance documentation included: DPA templates, audit logs, data processing records"
- "REST API with comprehensive documentation, SDKs for Python, Node.js, and Java, sandbox environment"
- "Enterprise support: <4 hour response time, dedicated success manager, quarterly business reviews"

### 4. Active Voice, Clear Subject
**Principle:** Who does what is always clear.

❌ **Don't (Passive):**
- "Calls are handled automatically"
- "Security is ensured through encryption"
- "Integration can be completed"

✅ **Do (Active):**
- "itellico.ai handles incoming calls automatically"
- "We encrypt all data at rest and in transit"
- "Your development team can complete integration in one sprint"

### 5. One Idea Per Sentence
**Principle:** Don't cram; clarity over density.

❌ **Don't:**
- "Our platform, which is GDPR-compliant and features natural-sounding voices with low latency, integrates easily with your existing systems while providing comprehensive analytics and reporting tools that help you understand customer interactions better and make data-driven decisions."

✅ **Do:**
- "Our platform is GDPR-compliant from day one. It features natural-sounding German voices with <2-second response times. Integration with your existing CRM takes one sprint. Comprehensive analytics help you understand customer interactions and make data-driven decisions."

## 🌍 German Language Guidelines

### Formal vs. Informal Address

**General Rule:** Use formal "Sie" for B2B enterprise communications.

**When to use "Sie" (formal):**
- Website copy (primary audience)
- Sales materials
- Contract/legal documents
- Initial customer communications
- Executive-level communications

**When "du" (informal) might be appropriate:**
- Developer community forums
- Informal team communications
- Startup/SMB audience (if explicitly requested)

**Default:** If unsure, always use "Sie"

---

### German-Specific Writing Principles

#### 1. Compound Words
German creates long compound words. Design layouts with flexibility.

**Examples:**
- Datenschutzgrundverordnung (GDPR)
- Geschäftsführung (management)
- Kundenbeziehungsmanagement (CRM)

**Writing tip:** Break complex terms into simpler components when possible, or use
commonly understood acronyms.

❌ "Unsere Sprachautomatisierungstechnologie..."
✅ "Unsere Voice-AI-Technologie..." (borrowed English term, clearer)

#### 2. Precision & Directness
German business communication values directness.

❌ **Don't (Too Flowery):**
"Wir würden uns sehr freuen, wenn Sie vielleicht Interesse haben könnten..."

✅ **Do (Direct):**
"Vereinbaren Sie jetzt einen Termin für eine Demo."

#### 3. Technical Terms
Many technical terms use English loanwords in German tech context.

**Keep in English:**
- API (not "Programmierschnittstelle")
- Dashboard (not "Instrumententafel")
- Call (not always "Anruf"—context dependent)
- Compliance (often used as-is)

**Use German:**
- Kunde (not "Customer")
- Datenschutz (not "Data Protection")
- Unternehmen (not "Business")

---

### Translation Quality Standards

**When translating English → German:**

1. **Don't use Google Translate alone**
   - Use professional translators or native speakers
   - Technical accuracy is critical
   - Cultural context matters

2. **Maintain brand voice**
   - Professional, precise, confident
   - Not overly formal or stiff
   - Match the tone of German enterprise software

3. **Test for length**
   - German text is ~30% longer than English
   - Check responsive layouts
   - Ensure buttons/CTAs fit

4. **Local terminology**
   - Use terms familiar in DACH region
   - Consider Swiss vs. German vs. Austrian variations
   - Industry-specific terminology

## 🚫 Words & Phrases to Avoid

### Banned Words (Too Hype-y)
- ❌ "Revolutionary"
- ❌ "Disruptive"
- ❌ "Game-changing"
- ❌ "Cutting-edge"
- ❌ "Next-generation"
- ❌ "World-class"
- ❌ "Innovative" (overused)
- ❌ "Leverage" (unless actually about leverage)
- ❌ "Synergy"
- ❌ "Unlock the power of"

### Banned Phrases (Too Vague)
- ❌ "Best-in-class solution"
- ❌ "Transform your business"
- ❌ "Take your [X] to the next level"
- ❌ "Seamless experience" (unless you can prove it)
- ❌ "Empower your team" (unless specific)
- ❌ "Drive results" (what results?)

### Red Flags (Requires Proof)
These words are OK *only if* backed by specific evidence:
- ⚠️ "Easy" → Show setup time or user feedback
- ⚠️ "Fast" → Provide latency metrics
- ⚠️ "Secure" → List certifications/audits
- ⚠️ "Reliable" → Show uptime statistics
- ⚠️ "Simple" → Demonstrate with example

## 🧪 Message Testing Framework

### Before Publishing, Ask:

1. **Is it true?**
   - Can we prove this claim?
   - Do we have data/testimonials to back it up?

2. **Is it clear?**
   - Would a non-expert understand this?
   - Is there a simpler way to say it?

3. **Is it specific?**
   - Did we use concrete numbers/examples?
   - Or is it vague marketing speak?

4. **Is it relevant?**
   - Does this matter to our audience?
   - Does it address a real pain point?

5. **Is it differentiated?**
   - Could a competitor say the same thing?
   - What makes this uniquely itellico.ai?

6. **Is it on-brand?**
   - Does it sound like us (professional, precise, confident)?
   - Or does it sound generic/corporate?

## 📝 Style Guide Quick Reference

### Numbers & Data
- ✅ Use numerals for all numbers (99%, 5 days, 1.2 seconds)
- ✅ Include units (seconds, euros, calls/minute)
- ✅ Use commas for thousands in English (10,000), periods in German (10.000)
- ✅ Percentage: use % symbol (99%)

### Capitalization
- ✅ Sentence case for headlines (except German nouns)
- ✅ Title Case for CTAs buttons ("Get Started", "Book Demo")
- ✅ Product features: lowercase unless proper noun
- ✅ German: capitalize all nouns

### Punctuation
- ✅ Oxford comma in lists (A, B, and C)
- ✅ Use em dashes for emphasis—like this
- ✅ One space after periods
- ✅ Avoid exclamation marks (maximum one per page)

### Abbreviations
- ✅ GDPR, API, CRM, SLA (no periods)
- ✅ Spell out on first use in long-form content
- ✅ Use common abbreviations in UI for space

### Links & CTAs
- ✅ Use descriptive link text ("View pricing" not "Click here")
- ✅ Button CTAs: action-oriented ("Get Started" not "Submit")
- ✅ Avoid "Learn more" (be more specific)
