## Overview

Tracking the right metrics is essential for understanding agent performance and driving improvements. This guide covers the most important metrics for voice AI agents, what they mean, target benchmarks, and how to improve them.

---

## Core Performance Metrics

### 1. Goal Achievement Rate

**What it measures:** Percentage of conversations where configured goals were achieved

**Formula:** (Goals Achieved / Total Goals Evaluated) × 100

**Where to find it:** Analytics → Goals or Conversation logs

**Target benchmarks:**
- ✅ Excellent: >85%
- 👍 Good: 70-85%
- ⚠️ Needs improvement: &lt;70%

**Why it matters:**
- Direct measure of agent effectiveness
- Indicates if agent accomplishes its purpose
- Correlates with customer satisfaction
- Reflects instruction quality

**How to improve:**
<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Review failed conversations" icon="magnifying-glass">
    - Read transcripts where goals weren't achieved
    - Look for patterns in failures
    - Identify knowledge gaps
    - Note instruction issues
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Refine goal definitions" icon="bullseye">
    - Make criteria more specific
    - Add context for edge cases
    - Align goals with actual success metrics
    - Test goal evaluation accuracy
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Improve agent capabilities" icon="wrench">
    - Add missing knowledge
    - Configure needed actions
    - Update instructions
    - Fix broken integrations
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Set realistic goals" icon="target">
    - Don't set unachievable goals
    - Break complex goals into steps
    - Consider goal hierarchy
    - Adjust for use case complexity
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### 2. First-Call Resolution (FCR) Rate

**What it measures:** Percentage of issues resolved in the first conversation without transfers or callbacks

**Formula:** (Conversations Resolved / Total Conversations) × 100

**Where to find it:** Goal analytics or post-call analysis

**Target benchmarks:**
- ✅ Excellent: >80%
- 👍 Good: 70-80%
- ⚠️ Needs improvement: &lt;70%

**Why it matters:**
- Lower operational costs (fewer repeat calls)
- Higher customer satisfaction
- Better resource utilization
- Indicates agent competence

**How to measure:**
Set up a goal like:
```
Goal: "Resolve customer issue"
Criteria: Customer's question was answered completely OR
their problem was solved AND customer confirmed resolution
AND no transfer to human was needed
```

**How to improve:**
<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Expand knowledge base" icon="book">
    - Add FAQs for common questions
    - Include troubleshooting guides
    - Document policies thoroughly
    - Keep information current
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Enable more actions" icon="bolt">
    - Add self-service actions
    - Integrate with more systems
    - Automate common tasks
    - Reduce "I'll need to transfer you" scenarios
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Improve instructions" icon="message">
    - Teach agent to ask clarifying questions
    - Guide through multi-step processes
    - Handle objections and edge cases
    - Provide troubleshooting steps
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### 3. Transfer Rate

**What it measures:** Percentage of conversations transferred to humans or other destinations

**Formula:** (Calls Transferred / Total Calls) × 100

**Where to find it:** Analytics dashboard or conversation logs filtered by transfers

**Target benchmarks:**
- ✅ Excellent: &lt;15%
- 👍 Good: 15-25%
- ⚠️ Needs improvement: >25%

**Why it matters:**
- High transfers = automation not working
- Increases operational costs
- May indicate poor agent design
- Shows scope limitations

**Types of transfers to track:**
- Transfers due to agent limitations
- Transfers due to customer request
- Transfers due to escalation
- Transfers due to errors

**How to improve:**
<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Analyze transfer reasons" icon="clipboard-list">
    - Review transcripts leading to transfers
    - Categorize why transfers happen
    - Identify most common transfer triggers
    - Prioritize highest-volume reasons
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Expand agent capabilities" icon="plus">
    - Add knowledge for common transfer reasons
    - Configure actions to handle requests
    - Improve instructions for edge cases
    - Train agent on escalation criteria
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Set clear transfer criteria" icon="filter">
    - Define when transfers are appropriate
    - Avoid premature transfers
    - Handle objections before transferring
    - Confirm transfer necessity
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Note>
Some transfers are appropriate and expected (e.g., legal questions, complex issues). The goal is to minimize unnecessary transfers, not eliminate all transfers.
</Note>

### 4. Average Call Duration

**What it measures:** Mean length of all conversations

**Formula:** Total Call Duration / Number of Calls

**Where to find it:** Analytics dashboard

**Target benchmarks:**
- Varies by use case
- Support: 2-4 minutes typically good
- Sales: 3-8 minutes
- Booking: 1-3 minutes
- Track trends more than absolute values

**Why it matters:**
- Longer calls = higher costs
- Very short calls may indicate failures
- Duration impacts capacity
- Balance efficiency with thoroughness

**How to interpret:**
- ⬆️ Increasing duration: Agent may be verbose, knowledge retrieval slow, or handling more complex issues
- ⬇️ Decreasing duration: Agent may be more efficient OR dropping calls early
- 📊 Stable duration: Consistent performance

**How to improve:**
<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Reduce unnecessary verbosity" icon="scissors">
    - Shorten agent responses
    - Remove repetitive phrases
    - Get to point faster
    - Use concise language in instructions
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Improve information retrieval" icon="bolt">
    - Optimize knowledge base for faster RAG
    - Use Context mode for small knowledge bases
    - Improve knowledge item titles
    - Remove irrelevant knowledge
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Streamline conversation flow" icon="route">
    - Ask for information upfront
    - Reduce back-and-forth
    - Combine questions when possible
    - Use more efficient actions
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Handle common cases faster" icon="forward-fast">
    - Create shortcuts for frequent scenarios
    - Provide quick answers for simple questions
    - Automate repetitive tasks
    - Reduce confirmation steps where safe
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### 5. Action Success Rate

**What it measures:** Percentage of triggered actions that execute successfully

**Formula:** (Successful Actions / Total Actions Attempted) × 100

**Where to find it:** Conversation logs with action data

**Target benchmarks:**
- ✅ Excellent: >95%
- 👍 Good: 90-95%
- ⚠️ Needs improvement: &lt;90%

**Why it matters:**
- Failed actions frustrate customers
- Indicate integration problems
- Impact goal achievement
- Reflect system reliability

**Common failure causes:**
- API endpoints down or slow
- Authentication failures
- Incorrect parameters
- Network timeouts
- Rate limiting

**How to improve:**
<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Monitor API health" icon="heartbeat">
    - Set up API monitoring
    - Track API response times
    - Get alerts for failures
    - Maintain backup endpoints
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Improve parameter extraction" icon="code">
    - Make extraction instructions clearer
    - Validate parameters before calling API
    - Provide examples in action descriptions
    - Handle missing data gracefully
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Add retry logic" icon="rotate">
    - Configure automatic retries for transient failures
    - Implement exponential backoff
    - Have fallback options
    - Inform user of retry attempts
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Better error handling" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    - Catch specific error types
    - Provide meaningful error messages to agent
    - Have agent communicate issues to user
    - Log detailed error information
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### 6. Cost Per Conversation

**What it measures:** Average cost to handle one complete conversation

**Formula:** Total Cost / Number of Conversations

**Where to find it:** Analytics dashboard with cost data enabled

**Typical ranges:**
- Simple Q&A: $0.10 - $0.30
- Support conversations: $0.20 - $0.50
- Complex sales calls: $0.40 - $1.00+

**Cost components:**
- **LLM inference** (40-60% of cost typically)
- **Text-to-Speech** (20-35%)
- **Speech-to-Text** (15-25%)
- **Knowledge base operations** (small %)

**Why it matters:**
- Calculate ROI vs human agents
- Budget forecasting
- Identify cost optimization opportunities
- Track cost trends over time

**How to improve:**
See the dedicated [Cost Optimization](/optimize/cost-optimization) guide.

### 7. Response Latency

**What it measures:** Time between user finishing speech and agent starting response

**Where to find it:** Conversation logs (timestamps between user and agent messages)

**Target benchmarks:**
- ✅ Excellent: &lt;1.5 seconds
- 👍 Good: 1.5-2.5 seconds
- ⚠️ Needs improvement: >2.5 seconds

**Why it matters:**
- High latency feels unnatural
- Frustrates users
- May cause interruptions
- Impacts conversation quality

**How to improve:**
<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Use faster models" icon="gauge">
    - GPT-4o-mini faster than GPT-4
    - Smaller models = faster inference
    - Consider provider latency differences
    - Test different model options
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Shorten prompts" icon="scissors">
    - Reduce instruction length
    - Move details to knowledge base
    - Remove unnecessary examples
    - Simplify context
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Optimize knowledge retrieval" icon="database">
    - Use RAG for large knowledge bases
    - Improve knowledge item titles
    - Remove irrelevant knowledge
    - Cache frequently accessed items
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Optimize actions" icon="bolt">
    - Make API calls faster
    - Use async where possible
    - Reduce payload sizes
    - Minimize action execution time
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Secondary Metrics

### Conversation Volume

**What it measures:** Total number of conversations over time

**Why track it:**
- Capacity planning
- Measure adoption
- Identify peak times
- Forecast costs

**Insights:**
- Upward trend: Growing usage, successful deployment
- Downward trend: Lower demand or issues
- Patterns: Peak hours, day of week trends, seasonal

---

### Completion Rate

**What it measures:** Percentage of conversations that complete normally (vs drop/error)

**Formula:** (Completed Calls / Total Calls) × 100

**Target:** >95%

**Low completion may indicate:**
- Technical issues
- Poor user experience
- Confusing conversation flow
- Long wait times

---

### Customer Sentiment

**What it measures:** Emotional tone of customer during conversation

**Where to find it:** Post-call analysis with sentiment questions

**Approaches:**
- Ask post-call: "Was the customer satisfied?" (Yes/No)
- Analyze language for sentiment indicators
- Track thanks/complaints in transcripts
- Monitor tone changes

**Use sentiment to:**
- Identify frustrating scenarios
- Measure quality improvements
- Spot training opportunities
- Correlate with other metrics

---

## Metric Combinations

Look at metrics together for deeper insights:

### Goal Achievement + Transfer Rate
- **Low goals, high transfers:** Agent can't handle scope
- **High goals, low transfers:** Excellent automation
- **Low both:** Fundamental issues

### Duration + Goal Achievement
- **Long duration, high goals:** Thorough but slow
- **Short duration, high goals:** Efficient and effective ✅
- **Long duration, low goals:** Struggling to help
- **Short duration, low goals:** Failing fast

### Action Success + Goal Achievement
- **High action success, high goals:** Actions driving success ✅
- **Low action success, low goals:** Broken integrations hurting outcomes
- **High action success, low goals:** Actions working but goals poorly defined

## Setting Up Your Metric Dashboard

<Steps>
  <Step title="Identify your use case">
    Different use cases prioritize different metrics:

    **Support:** FCR, Goal Achievement, Transfer Rate
    **Sales:** Goal Achievement, Duration, Sentiment
    **Booking:** Goal Achievement, Action Success, Duration
  </Step>

  <Step title="Establish baselines">
    - Run for 1-2 weeks
    - Record initial metrics
    - Don't optimize yet, just measure
    - Understand current performance
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set targets">
    - Use benchmarks from this guide
    - Adjust for your use case
    - Set realistic improvement goals
    - Plan incremental progress
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create review cadence">
    - Daily: Quick check for anomalies
    - Weekly: Review trends and issues
    - Monthly: Deep dive and reporting
    - Quarterly: Strategic planning
  </Step>

  <Step title="Take action">
    - Identify lowest-performing metric
    - Implement improvements
    - Measure impact
    - Iterate
  </Step>
</Steps>
